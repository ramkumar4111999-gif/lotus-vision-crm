6. FIRST FLOOR VISIBILITY CHALLENGES - DETAILED ANALYSIS
============================================================
CRITICAL ISSUE: The optical shop is on the FIRST FLOOR and described as
"somewhat hidden." This is the SINGLE BIGGEST DRAWBACK of this location.

A. Visibility Problems:
   - NO street-level signage visibility from passing traffic
   - Pedestrians on Mela Bazar street cannot see the shop directly
   - No window display opportunity (first floor = no ground-level windows)
   - Signboard on building exterior may be the ONLY marker
   - "Somewhat hidden" description confirms poor natural visibility

B. Access Barriers:
   - Stair climbing required - eliminates elderly customers, families with
     small children, and differently-abled persons
   - In small-town Tamil Nadu, many people PREFER ground-level shopping
   - First floor is perceived as "not easily accessible"
   - No elevator (unlikely in this building type/area)

C. Footfall Reduction Estimates:
   - Ground floor shops in Indian retail get 60-80% of walk-in traffic
   - First floor shops typically get only 20-40% of potential walk-ins
   - For "hidden" first floor locations, this drops to 5-15%
   - In Tier-3/4 towns, the preference for ground floor is EVEN STRONGER
     due to traditional shopping habits

D. Customer Psychology in Small-Town India:
   - Shoppers in Sankarankovil are habituated to street-level browsing
   - First floor shops feel "intimidating" or "exclusive" to walk-in
   - Without a strong brand or referral, first floor = unknown = avoided
   - Trust factor is lower for hidden/upper-floor establishments
   - Impulse walk-ins (critical for optical retail) are nearly ZERO

E. Competitive Disadvantage:
   - Any ground-floor optical shop automatically captures all the walk-in
     traffic that this location misses
   - Customers visiting Mela Bazar for groceries are unlikely to detour
     upstairs to browse optical products
   - Government office visitors (opposite) will not notice the shop
