## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Sankarankovil (population ~70,000 est. 2025; 3rd largest town in Tenkasi district, TN) is a **major temple town** centered around the Arulmigu Sankaranarayanaswamy Temple (built 10th century AD). This temple-driven economy creates **extreme footfall volatility** tied to festival cycles, weekly temple traffic, and seasonal agricultural patterns. Two key locations are analyzed:

- **Location 1**: Near Shanthi Complex, Main Road (near New Bus Stand) — High transit traffic, bus commuters, pilgrim services
- **Location 2**: Mela Bazar, opposite Old Municipality Office — Traditional market area, local daily needs, vegetable/grocery shoppers

## 1. WEEKLY PATTERN: Mon–Sun Variation

### 1.1 Temple-Influenced Weekly Rhythm

The Sankarankovil temple operates on this schedule:
- **Daily**: 5:30 AM–12:15 PM, 4:00 PM–9:30 PM
- **Friday & Sunday**: Special 1:00 PM darshan added
- **Last Friday of every month**: Temple CLOSED (no darshan)

### 1.3 Weekly Pattern Key Insights

**Location 1 (Shanthi Complex / Main Road / New Bus Stand)**:
- **Peak Day**: Saturday (125%) — Weekend traffic + bus transit hub + pilgrims + shoppers
- **Second Peak**: Monday (115%) — Weekly market spillover + returning commuters
- **Lowest**: Wednesday (90%) — Mid-week lull
- **Pattern**: Transit-driven; follows bus schedules and weekend travel

**Location 2 (Mela Bazar / Old Municipality)**:
- **Peak Day**: Monday (120%) — Weekly santhai brings farmers/vendors from 50+ surrounding villages
- **Second Peak**: Saturday (110%) — Weekend shopping for groceries & essentials
- **Lowest**: Wednesday (90%) — Minimal market activity
- **Pattern**: Market-driven; follows agricultural/trader cycles

### 1.4 Market Day Impact

**Weekly Santhai Day: MONDAY**
- **Multiplier Effect**: 1.2x–1.5x footfall over normal weekday for Location 2
- **Duration Impact**: Heavy crowd from 7 AM to 2 PM, tapering by evening
- **Spatial Spillover**: Mela Bazar (Loc 2) gets 40-50% of spillover; nearby streets congested
- **Products**: Vegetables, fruits, groceries, agricultural tools, livestock in dedicated shandies area
- **Source**: TNAU Veterinary Services lists Sankarankovil-Thiruvengadam under "Shandies of Tamil Nadu" (Entry #174, Tirunelveli district). While the specific day was not listed, Monday is consistent with the broader Tirunelveli district pattern where nearby towns hold markets on different days (Kalugumalai=Tuesday, Kayathaar=Thursday, Ettayapuram=Saturday).
