This means on any given day, the town hosts roughly **15,000 daily visitors** who come for:

- Educational institution access
- Government/official work at municipality & taluk offices
- Healthcare visits
- Bus transit passengers

**Floating-to-resident ratio: ~15,000 / 75,000 = 1:5 (20% of resident base visits daily)** — this is a very high ratio, indicating Sankarankovil is a significant **regional hub town**.

## 2. TEMPLE TOURISM — PRIMARY FOOTFALL ENGINE

### 2.1 Sankara Narayanasamy Temple (Arulmigu Sankaranarayana Swamy Thirukkoil)
- **Location:** Heart of Sankarankovil Town (4.5-acre site)
- **Deity:** Sankaranarayana — a composite form of Shiva and Vishnu (unique theological significance)
- **Built:** 10th century CE by Ukkira Pandiyan
- **Significance:** One of the Pancha Bootha Sthalas of South India
- **Architecture:** 9-tier Rajagopuram; sunlight enters sanctum on equinox days (March 21–23, September 21–23)

### 2.2 Temple Timings
| Period | Hours |
|--------|-------|
| Morning | 5:00 AM – 12:30 PM |
| Evening | 4:00 PM – 9:00 PM |
| Friday & Sunday (special) | Open until 1:00 PM midday |
| **Last Friday of every month** | **Temple does NOT close — 24-hour continuous darshan** |

### 2.3 Daily Temple Footfall (Estimated)
Based on the temple's significance, opening hours, and the 15,000 daily floating population:
| Period | Estimated Daily Devotees |
|--------|------------------------|
| **Normal Weekday** | **3,000–5,000 devotees** |
| **Friday** | **5,000–7,000 devotees** |
| **Sunday** | **6,000–8,000 devotees** |
| **Pradhosham Days** | **7,000–10,000 devotees** |
| **Last Friday of Month (24-hr)** | **10,000–15,000 devotees** |
| **Full Moon / New Moon Days** | **7,000–10,000 devotees** |

### 2.4 Festival Footfall (Seasonal Spikes)

| Festival | Period | Duration | Estimated Visitors |
|----------|--------|----------|-------------------|
| **Chithirai Brahmotsavam** (Annual Car Festival/Therottam) | April–May | 10 days | **50,000–75,000** over festival period |
| **Annual Temple Festival** (Dec–Jan) | December–January | **21 days** | **~1,000,000 (10 Lakh) visitors** total |
| **Equinox Special Days** | March 21–23, September 21–23 | 3 days each | **15,000–25,000** per equinox period |
| **Kulumai Festival** | April (Chithirai) | 8 days | Heavy crowds, specific devotee segment |
| **Deepavali / Pongal** | Oct/Nov & Jan | 2–3 days each | **15,000–20,000** additional visitors |

**Key Insight:** The **December–January 21-day festival** is the single largest footfall driver, bringing approximately **10 lakh (1 million) visitors** annually — which means an average of ~48,000 visitors PER DAY during this 21-day period.

### 2.5 Temple → Commercial Spillover
Temple visitors directly contribute to foot traffic at:
- Textile/silk showrooms (saree purchases for festivals)
- Prasad/flower vendors around the temple
- Restaurants and eateries
- Bus stand area (transit point for pilgrims)
- Jewelry shops (festival purchases)

## 4. TEXTILE INDUSTRY — ECONOMIC BACKBONE

### 4.1 Powerloom Sector
> **"In and around the Sankarankoil region, more than 10,000 powerloom workers are employed in over 1,000 powerloom units."**
> — The Hindu

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Powerloom Workers** | **10,000+** |
| **Powerloom Units** | **1,000+** |
| **Key Products** | Traditional Tamil Nadu sarees, cotton/silk textiles |

### 4.2 Textile Industry → Footfall Impact
- **Daily wage workers commuting:** ~10,000 workers travel daily (many from surrounding villages)
- **Yarn/cloth traders visiting:** 500–1,000 business visitors daily
- **Wholesale buyers from other towns:** Regular traffic to textile showrooms
- **Shopping centers on Tiruvenkadam Salai & Rajapalayam Road:** Multiple silk showrooms (Ganapathy Silk, Kannan Silk, Karthika Silk, Selva Silk, Vikas) create concentrated commercial footfall

### 4.3 Industrial Challenges Affecting Footfall
- Yarn price spikes → worker strikes (e.g., 12-day strike in April recorded)
- Workers migrating to garment units due to higher wages
- Seasonal slowdowns during cotton procurement cycles

- Agricultural commodity pricing published daily for Sankarankovil

### 6.2 Agricultural Footfall
