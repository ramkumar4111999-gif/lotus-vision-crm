SECTION 2: OPTICAL SHOP CUSTOMER BEHAVIOR IN SMALL TAMIL NADU TOWNS
================================================================================

2.1 WHO BUYS SPECTACLES AND WHY?
---------------------------------
Based on research findings:

A) SCHOOL CHILDREN (Ages 6-18) — Growing Segment
   - Myopia prevalence in Tamil Nadu school children: 7-17.5%
     (Source: Multiple studies on South Indian school children, PMC)
   - In Chidambaram, TN specifically: 12.9% myopia prevalence found
   - In Tamil Nadu public schools: Steep increase in myopia observed
   - Tamil Nadu Government provides FREE spectacles to school students
     (Source: NHSRC Tamil Nadu School Eye Screening Program)
   - Despite free government spectacles, many parents prefer private optical
     shops for better frame selection, quality, and faster service
   - Children need: Distance glasses (myopia), reading glasses, safety glasses

B) YOUNG ADULTS (18-35) — Largest Segment
   - Digital eye strain from mobile phones/computers is a major driver
   - Fashion-conscious — prefer branded frames and sunglasses
   - Contact lens users — regular replacements needed
   - This is the HIGHEST revenue per customer segment

C) MIDDLE-AGED (35-55) — Presbyopia Onset
   - Reading glasses needed (presbyopia typically starts at 40-45 years)
   - Progressives and bifocals — higher average transaction value
   - Computer glasses increasingly in demand
   - In rural South India, 28.50% had vision less than standard
     (Source: PMC TREE Study, rural South India)

D) ELDERLY (55+) — High Need, Low Awareness
   - Cataract prevalence in South India: 71.8% in elderly
     (Source: AAO Journal, India cataract study)
   - 84.46% of rural South Indian adults have NEVER had an eye examination
     (Source: PMC study on eye care seeking behavior)
   - Need: Reading glasses, post-cataract spectacles, low vision aids
   - This represents an UNTAPPED market in small towns

2.2 WHY PEOPLE VISIT OPTICAL SHOPS (Purchase Triggers)
-------------------------------------------------------
1. Eye irritation/headache complaints → leads to eye checkup
2. School screening referral (children)
3. Broken/lost spectacles (replacement/urgent)
4. Fashion/cosmetic desire (sunglasses, new frames)
5. Annual prescription change
6. Doctor referral from eye hospital

2.3 PURCHASE DECISION FACTORS IN SMALL TOWNS
----------------------------------------------
- TRUST and VISIBILITY are the #1 factors
- Recommendation by neighbors/word-of-mouth is dominant
- LOCATION on a known road/landmark matters enormously
- Price sensitivity is HIGH but willingness to pay for quality exists
- Ground floor accessibility = perceived legitimacy
- Optical shops on Main Road = "established" perception

SECTION 3: GROUND FLOOR vs FIRST FLOOR — CRITICAL IMPACT ANALYSIS
================================================================================

3.1 RESEARCH EVIDENCE
---------------------
- Ground floor shops receive 60-70% MORE walk-in traffic than first floor
  (Source: Indian retail industry reports, Vedatam.com analysis)
- Road-facing ground floor units command premium rents precisely because of
  this footfall differential
- Eye-level products sell 35% more than products on top/bottom shelves
  (Source: VCS Builders retail psychology study) — this principle extends to
  store placement: ground floor = eye-level for pedestrians
- In Indian retail, high street ground floor conversion rates are HIGHER than
  malls (Source: India Retailing, Ginesys studies)
- The premium for road-facing ground floor vs upper floor is 40-80% in rent,
  justified by footfall differential

3.2 WHY THIS MATTERS FOR AN OPTICAL SHOP
------------------------------------------
- Optical shops are a "considered purchase" — customers don't impulse-buy
  spectacles daily
- HOWEVER, first-time discovery and brand awareness depend heavily on visibility
- A person walking past a ground floor optical shop daily will REMEMBER it when
  they need spectacles
- First floor shops are "invisible" to 80%+ of street traffic
- In a small town where word-of-mouth is key, the ground floor shop gets 3-4x
  more daily visual impressions, leading to more recommendations
- Elderly customers (major optical shop demographic) STRUGGLE with stairs —
  first floor is a significant barrier for presbyopia/cataract patients

3.3 SPECIFIC IMPACT ESTIMATE FOR OPTICAL SHOPS
-----------------------------------------------
  Ground Floor Walk-ins: 100% (baseline)
  First Floor Walk-ins: 30-40% of ground floor equivalent
  
  This means a first floor optical shop can expect to lose 60-70% of potential
  walk-in customers compared to an equivalent ground floor location.
