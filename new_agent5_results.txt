SECTION 2: FOOTFALL ESTIMATION METHODOLOGY
================================================================================

  Method: Bottom-up estimation using floating population allocation

  Step 1: Distribute 15,000 daily floating population across commercial areas
  Step 2: Add local resident daily shopping trips
  Step 3: Apply location-specific adjustments (floor level, visibility)
  Step 4: Apply deduplication factor for overlapping traffic

  Key Multipliers (sourced from Indian retail research):
  - Ground Floor vs 1st Floor: 60-70% reduction in walk-in traffic
    (Source: Vedatam.com, Indian retail industry studies)
  - Main Road vs Interior Market: 2.5-3.5x more total foot traffic
    (Source: Knight Frank High Streets Report, JLL research)
  - Indian retail store conversion rate: 20-30% of walk-ins
    (Source: Retailers Association of India)

  LOCATION: M2square Mobiles, 15B, Mela Bazar, Shankar Nagar,
            opposite Old Municipality Office — FIRST FLOOR, INTERIOR

  MELA BAZAR AREA ANALYSIS:
  ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
  Mela Bazar is a traditional interior market area in Sankarankovil,
  distinct from the main Thiruvenkadam Salai commercial corridor.

  CATEGORY A: FLOATING POPULATION VISITING MELA BAZAR
  ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
  - Of 15,000 daily floating population, Mela Bazar (as a secondary
    interior market) captures approximately 15-20%
  - Main Road (Thiruvenkadam Salai) captures ~45-50%
  - Remaining floating population goes to other areas/temple/transit
  - Estimated: 2,250–3,000 persons visit Mela Bazaar area/day

  CATEGORY B: MUNICIPALITY OFFICE VISITORS
  ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
  - Old Municipality Office (nearby) brings government-related traffic
  - Estimated 50-100 visitors/day (NOT shopping traffic — these visitors
    are doing government work, extremely unlikely to browse shops)
  - Net retail-relevant footfall from this: negligible (5-15 people)

  CATEGORY C: LOCAL RESIDENT DAILY SHOPPING
  ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
  - Shankar Nagar residents shopping for daily needs
  - Estimated: 500–1,200 persons/day visiting Mela Bazaar shops

  CATEGORY D: PURPOSE-DRIVEN MARKET SHOPPERS
  ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
  - Traditional bazaar shoppers (textiles, groceries, hardware)
  - Estimated: 300–800 persons/day
  - Many are repeat, loyal customers of existing shops

  CATEGORY E: PASSING THROUGH TRAFFIC
  ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
  - Unlike Main Road, Mela Bazaar has minimal through-traffic
  - People come here specifically to shop, not passing through
  - Estimated: 100–300 persons/day incidental passersby

  MELA BAZAR GROUND FLOOR AREA FOOTFALL:
  ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
  Sum of categories A-E (before first floor reduction):
    LOW:    3,205 persons/day
    MED:    4,200 persons/day
    HIGH:   5,400 persons/day

  After deduplication (factor: 0.80 — less overlap than Main Road):
    LOW:    ~2,560 persons/day (ground floor)
    MED:    ~3,360 persons/day (ground floor)
    HIGH:   ~4,320 persons/day (ground floor)

  FIRST FLOOR REDUCTION:
  ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
  Research establishes that first floor shops receive 60-70% FEWER
  walk-in visitors than ground floor equivalents in Indian retail:

  Evidence:
  1. Vedatam.com ground floor vs first floor analysis
  2. Indian retail reports: road-facing ground floor commands 40-80%
     rent premium precisely due to footfall differential
  3. The Hindu Business Line: "If the shop is not on ground or first
     floor, focus gets deviated"
  4. Retail psychology: ground floor = eye-level for pedestrians
     (same principle as eye-level shelf placement selling 35% more)

  Additional factors for THIS specific first floor location:
  - "Somewhat hidden" — poor signage visibility from street
  - Narrow/staircase access typical of old bazaar buildings
  - No elevator (unlikely in tier-4 town old market building)
  - Staircase entrance not prominent from the street
  - Interior market = less natural foot traffic to begin with
  - No landmark anchor (Old Municipality Office is NOT a shopping draw)

  Applying 65-75% reduction for first floor + hidden interior:

  FIRST FLOOR AREA FOOTFALL (Location 1):
  ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
    LOW:    2,560 × 0.25 = 640 persons/day
    MED:    3,360 × 0.30 = 1,008 persons/day
    HIGH:   4,320 × 0.35 = 1,512 persons/day

SECTION 6: REASONS FOR THE FUNDAMENTAL DIFFERENCE
================================================================================

  The ~7.4x footfall difference between the two locations is driven by
  FIVE compounding disadvantages at Location 1:

  REASON 1: MAIN ROAD vs INTERIOR MARKET (2.2x factor)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  - Thiruvenkadam Salai (Location 2) is the PRIMARY commercial artery
    of Sankarankovil — it connects the bus stand, temple, silk showrooms,
    shopping complexes, schools, and banks.
  - Mela Bazar (Location 1) is a SECONDARY interior market — people
    come here only for specific traditional shopping needs.
  - In Indian tier-3/4 towns, the main road captures 2.2-3.5x the
    foot traffic of interior market areas (JLL, Knight Frank research).
  - The main road has PASSING THROUGH traffic (people walking to other
    destinations), while Mela Bazar only has DESTINATION traffic.

  REASON 2: GROUND FLOOR vs FIRST FLOOR (2.5-3.3x factor)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  - Ground floor shops receive 60-70% MORE walk-in traffic in India
    (Vedatam.com, retail industry standard).
  - First floor shops are invisible to 80%+ of street-level pedestrians.
  - In a town with no elevators, stairs are a physical barrier —
    especially for elderly customers and families with children.
  - The "somewhat hidden" nature of this specific first floor shop
    compounds the problem — even people who know the building exist
    may not notice the shop entrance.
  - Small-town retail psychology: ground floor = "established business"
    while first floor = "struggling/starting up" perception.

  REASON 3: ANCHOR & LANDMARK EFFECT (1.5-2x factor)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  - Location 2 is opposite Shanthi Complex — an established
    commercial anchor that draws 400-900 daily visitors.
  - Location 2 is near the New Bus Stand — the #1 transit hub.
  - Location 2 is on the approach road to the famous temple.
  - Location 2 has multiple silk showrooms as complementary anchors.
  - Location 1 is opposite the Old Municipality Office — a government
    building that does NOT draw shopping-oriented footfall.
  - Location 1 has no commercial anchor nearby.

  REASON 4: SCHOOL ZONE TRAFFIC (exclusive to Location 2)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  - Sengundar School generates 750-1,200 daily passersby
    (students + parents at drop-off and pick-up).
  - This creates two daily traffic peaks that are predictable and
    consistent regardless of season or market conditions.
  - Location 1 has NO school or educational institution nearby.

  REASON 5: BUS TRANSIT CONNECTIVITY (exclusive to Location 2)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  - 250-300 bus movements/day with 10,000-14,000 passengers.
  - 45-55% of bus passengers walk past Location 2's spot.
  - This alone contributes 3,150-5,390 daily passersby — MORE than
    the ENTIRE estimated footfall at Location 1.
  - Location 1 has NO bus stand, bus stop, or transit connectivity.

  COMPOUNDING EFFECT:
  ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
  These five factors MULTIPLY together (not just add):
  Main Road (2.2x) × Ground Floor (2.8x) × Anchor Effect (1.7x) ×
  School + Transit (bonus) = approximately 7-8x total advantage
