## 1. Executive Summary

Sankaran Kovil Opticals is a well-established optical retail shop serving the people of Sankarankovil and surrounding areas in Tamil Nadu, India. As the region's digital adoption accelerates — driven by affordable smartphones and Reliance Jio's 4G/5G penetration — the shop's future growth depends on building a credible, discoverable, and user-friendly digital presence. This comprehensive plan outlines every aspect of creating, launching, and maintaining a professional website tailored specifically to the needs of an optical shop in a Tier-3/4 Tamil Nadu market.

The website will serve three primary objectives: **(1)** Establish digital credibility and trust so that potential customers searching for "optical shop near me" or "eye doctor Sankarankovil" find a polished, professional online storefront; **(2)** Drive foot traffic to the physical store through online appointment booking for eye exams, product previews, and store directions; and **(3)** Generate leads and enquiries through WhatsApp integration, contact forms, and social media connectivity. Unlike large e-commerce optical retailers (Lenskart, Titan Eye+), the website will primarily function as a digital brochure and local SEO engine rather than a full-fledged e-commerce platform — though a phased approach to online ordering for contact lenses and accessories is recommended.

This plan was developed after extensive research into best practices from leading optical retailers worldwide (including Fittingbox, Zenni Optical, Warby Parker), analysis of website builders suitable for Indian small businesses (Wix, Shopify, Dukaan, WordPress/WooCommerce), and evaluation of emerging technologies such as AR-based virtual try-on and WhatsApp Business API appointment booking. Every recommendation has been calibrated for the shop's budget constraints, the local market's digital literacy levels, and the technical infrastructure available in Sankarankovil.

## 2. Website Requirements

### 2.1 Core Functional Requirements

The website for Sankaran Kovil Opticals must fulfil a carefully prioritised set of functional requirements. These requirements have been categorised into three tiers based on business impact and implementation complexity:

**Tier 1 — Must-Have (Launch Phase):**
- **Multi-page informational website** with at least six core pages (Home, Products, Eye Exam Booking, About Us, Contact, Blog) — this forms the backbone of the digital presence.
- **Mobile-responsive design** that renders perfectly on devices ranging from low-cost Android phones (common in Tier-3/4 markets) to desktop monitors. Given that over 85% of Indian internet users access the web via mobile, this is non-negotiable.
- **Eye exam appointment booking form** integrated with WhatsApp notifications — allowing customers to book appointments online and receive instant confirmation on their preferred messaging platform.
- **Embedded Google Maps** showing the shop's precise location in Sankarankovil, along with driving directions from nearby landmarks (bus stand, temple, hospital).
- **Product showcase gallery** displaying the shop's eyewear collections (spectacle frames, sunglasses, contact lenses) with high-quality images, brand names, pricing ranges, and category filters.
- **WhatsApp floating button** on every page for instant customer communication — this is the single most important conversion tool for Indian small businesses.
- **Contact information** prominently displayed: phone number, WhatsApp number, email, physical address, and operating hours.
- **SEO-optimised content** targeting local search queries such as "optical shop in Sankarankovil," "eye test near me," "spectacle frames Sankarankovil," and "best optician Tirunelveli district."
- **Fast page loading times** (under 3 seconds on 4G connections) — critical because average internet speeds in smaller Tamil Nadu towns can fluctuate between 5–20 Mbps.

**Tier 2 — Should-Have (Phase 2, Post-Launch 3–6 Months):**
- **Bilingual content support** (Tamil and English) — essential for reaching the broader local population, especially older customers and those from rural areas surrounding Sankarankovil.
- **Blog section** with articles on eye health, lens technology, frame styling tips, and seasonal promotions to improve organic search rankings and establish authority.
- **Customer testimonials and reviews section** featuring photos of satisfied customers (with consent) wearing their new glasses from the shop.
- **Online enquiry form** for customers to ask about specific products, lens types, or pricing before visiting the store.
- **Insurance/payment partner information** — listing EMI options, insurance partnerships (if any), and accepted payment methods (UPI, cash, card).
- **Promotional banners and offers carousel** on the home page to highlight seasonal discounts (Diwali sale, back-to-school offers, Pongal promotions).

**Tier 3 — Nice-to-Have (Phase 3, 6–12 Months):**
- **Basic e-commerce functionality** for ordering contact lenses, lens cleaning solutions, and accessories with local delivery within Sankarankovil.
- **Virtual try-on feature** using AR technology or photo upload — allowing customers to preview how frames look on their face before visiting the shop.
- **Prescription upload facility** — allowing customers to upload their eye prescription for quick lens fitting when they visit the store.
- **Customer loyalty programme integration** — tracking visits and purchases to reward repeat customers.
- **Live chat support** — either human-powered during business hours or AI chatbot for after-hours queries.

### 2.2 Non-Functional Requirements

- **Performance:** Page load time under 3 seconds on 4G; under 1.5 seconds on WiFi. Core Web Vitals (LCP, FID, CLS) must pass Google's threshold for "Good" rating.
- **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance, including proper alt text for images, sufficient colour contrast, keyboard navigation, and screen reader compatibility. This is especially important for an optical shop's website, as visually impaired users may access it.
- **Security:** SSL certificate (HTTPS), secure form submissions, GDPR/India DPDPA 2023 compliant data handling for customer information and prescriptions.
- **Scalability:** Architecture must support adding e-commerce features, virtual try-on, and multilingual content without requiring a complete rebuild.
- **Browser Compatibility:** Full support for Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Samsung Internet (the dominant browser on budget Android phones in India).

## 4. Key Website Pages

### 4.1 Home Page Design Brief

The home page is the digital storefront — it must instantly communicate trust, professionalism, and the shop's unique value proposition to visitors arriving from Google search, social media links, or word-of-mouth referrals. Research into top-performing optical websites (from Fittingbox, Colorlib, iMatrix, and DesignCrowd analyses) reveals that the most effective home pages combine hero imagery, clear calls-to-action, social proof, and product visibility in a clean, uncluttered layout.

**Hero Section (Above the Fold):**
The hero section occupies the full viewport on both mobile and desktop and features a high-quality photograph of a diverse customer wearing stylish eyeglasses, shot in the actual Sankaran Kovil Opticals shop interior (to establish authenticity). The overlay text reads: "See the World Clearly — Trusted Eye Care in Sankarankovil Since [Year]." Two prominent CTA buttons appear: "Book Your Eye Exam" (primary, green accent) and "Explore Our Collections" (secondary, outlined). On mobile, these buttons stack vertically for easy tapping. A subtle parallax scrolling effect adds depth without impacting performance. A small trust badge strip below the hero shows "20+ Years of Service | 10,000+ Happy Customers | Certified Optometrist."

**Services Overview Strip:**
Immediately below the hero, a horizontal strip (or stacked cards on mobile) showcases the shop's core services with icon + short text: Comprehensive Eye Exams, Premium Eyewear Collections, Contact Lens Fitting, Children's Eye Care, and Lens Repair & Adjustment. Each card links to the relevant page.

**Featured Collections Carousel:**
A visually rich section displays the shop's top-selling or newest frame collections. Each product card includes a high-resolution image, brand name, frame type tag (e.g., "Titanium," "Acetate"), starting price (e.g., "From ₹999"), and a "View Details" link. Auto-scrolling on desktop with swipe gestures on mobile. Maximum 8–10 products to avoid overwhelming the visitor.

**Why Choose Us Section:**
Four to six key differentiators presented in a grid layout with icons and brief descriptions: "Expert Optometrists," "Wide Range of Brands," "Affordable Pricing," "Same-Day Service," "Free Frame Adjustment," and "Insurance Accepted." This section builds trust and addresses common customer concerns about choosing a local optical shop over online retailers.

**Customer Testimonials:**
A rotating carousel of 3–5 customer testimonials, each featuring a customer photo (with consent), name, locality (e.g., "Ramesh, Sankarankovil"), and a short quote about their experience. This social proof is critical for converting sceptical first-time visitors.

**Eye Health Blog Preview:**
The latest 2–3 blog posts displayed as cards with thumbnail images, titles, and brief excerpts — driving traffic to the blog and improving SEO through fresh content.

**Promotional Banner:**
A visually distinct banner highlighting the current offer (e.g., "Free Eye Exam with Every Frame Purchase — Limited Time Offer") with a CTA button linking to the booking page.

**Location & Hours Strip:**
A compact section showing the shop's address, phone number, Google Maps pin, and operating hours — giving visitors all the information needed to visit the store without navigating to a separate page.

**Footer:**
Comprehensive footer containing navigation links, social media icons, WhatsApp link, Google Maps embed, and copyright information.

### 4.2 Products / Collections Page

The Products page is the digital equivalent of walking through the shop's display shelves. It must present the full range of eyewear in an organised, visually appealing, and easily navigable manner. Research from eyewear e-commerce leaders like Zenni Optical, Lenskart, and Warby Parker shows that effective product pages combine powerful filtering, high-quality imagery, and informative descriptions to reduce decision fatigue and drive in-store visits.

**Page Layout:**
The Products page opens with a category navigation bar at the top: Eyeglasses | Sunglasses | Contact Lenses | Lenses | Accessories. Below this, a sidebar filter panel (collapsible on mobile) and a product grid (3 columns on desktop, 2 on tablet, 1 on mobile) make up the main content area.

**Filtering & Sorting:**
Customers can filter products by: Brand (Ray-Ban, Titan, Vincent Chase, Polaroid, Fastrack, etc.), Frame Shape (Round, Rectangular, Aviator, Cat-Eye, Wayfarer, Oval), Frame Material (Metal, Acetate, Titanium, TR90), Gender (Men, Women, Kids, Unisex), Price Range (slider or predefined ranges: Under ₹500, ₹500–1000, ₹1000–2000, ₹2000–5000, Above ₹5000), and Colour. Sorting options include: Price (Low to High / High to Low), Newest First, Best Selling, and Brand A–Z.

**Product Cards:**
Each product card displays: high-resolution frame image (front view, with 2–3 additional views on hover or swipe), brand name, model name/number, frame type tag, starting price, available colours (as small colour swatches), and a "View Details" or "Enquire on WhatsApp" button. For a non-e-commerce Phase 1 launch, the CTA buttons direct customers to enquire via WhatsApp or visit the store rather than "Add to Cart."

**Product Detail Page (PDP):**
Clicking a product card opens a detailed view with: multiple high-resolution images (front, side, angled, on-model), full product description (frame dimensions, material, weight, suitable face shapes), available colour options, price range, brand story (brief), lens compatibility information, and a prominent "Enquire Now" button that opens a pre-filled WhatsApp message. A "Book Fitting Appointment" button links to the eye exam booking page.

**Collection Pages:**
Seasonal or curated collections (e.g., "Summer Sunglasses Collection 2025," "Back-to-School Eyewear," "Premium Titanium Frames") can be created as special landing pages with editorial-style layouts, lifestyle imagery, and curated product selections. These pages serve as excellent content for social media promotion and email marketing campaigns.

**Inventory Management Note:**
Since this is a physical retail shop with limited inventory, the website should clearly indicate "Available in-store" or "Visit to check availability" rather than showing real-time stock levels. For popular items, a "Reserve This Frame" feature can allow customers to hold an item for 24 hours via WhatsApp confirmation.

### 4.3 Eye Exam Booking Page

The Eye Exam Booking page is arguably the highest-conversion page on the website, as it directly translates digital traffic into in-store footfall and revenue. Research into optometry practice websites (from Colorlib's 24 Best Optometrist Website Examples and Ocuco's e-commerce for opticians guide) shows that the most effective booking pages are simple, reassuring, and friction-free.

**Page Hero Section:**
A calming, professional header image (perhaps the optometrist at work or the eye examination equipment) with the heading "Book Your Comprehensive Eye Examination" and a supporting line: "Professional eye care by certified optometrists. Walk-in or book online — your vision is our priority."

**Booking Form Design:**
The appointment form is the centrepiece of this page and must be designed for maximum completion rates. Fields include:

1. **Full Name** (required) — text input
2. **Phone Number** (required) — with Indian mobile number validation (10 digits, +91 prefix)
3. **Email Address** (optional) — for confirmation and follow-up
4. **Preferred Date** (required) — date picker showing available slots for the next 14 days; weekends highlighted differently; blocked dates (holidays, closed days) greyed out
5. **Preferred Time** (required) — dropdown or visual time-slot selector showing available 30-minute slots (e.g., 9:00 AM, 9:30 AM ... 7:00 PM); slots already booked shown as "Filled"
6. **Type of Examination** (required) — radio buttons or cards: Routine Eye Check-up, Comprehensive Eye Exam (with dilation), Contact Lens Fitting, Children's Eye Exam, Diabetic Eye Screening
7. **Previous Prescription Upload** (optional) — file upload for customers who have an existing prescription
8. **Special Notes** (optional) — textarea for any specific concerns

**Confirmation & Communication Flow:**
Upon submission, the customer sees a confirmation screen displaying their appointment details, plus three communication touchpoints:
- **WhatsApp message** sent automatically to the customer's phone with appointment details, shop address, and a Google Maps link — using the WhatsApp Business API or a pre-configured message template via a service like Interakt or Wappbiz.
- **SMS notification** as a backup for customers who may not use WhatsApp (though rare in Tamil Nadu).
- **Shop notification** — the shop owner/staff receive an alert (via WhatsApp, SMS, or email) with the new appointment details.

**Cancellation & Rescheduling:**
The confirmation message includes links to reschedule or cancel the appointment. A simple form allows date/time changes up to 2 hours before the appointment.

**Pricing & Insurance Information:**
Below the form, a clear section lists examination types and their costs (e.g., "Routine Eye Check-up: ₹200," "Comprehensive Eye Exam: ₹500"), accepted payment methods, and any insurance partnerships (e.g., "We accept Star Health, New India Assurance, and other major insurance plans for eye examinations").

**FAQ Accordion:**
Common questions addressed: "How long does an eye exam take?" (20–40 minutes), "Do I need to bring anything?" (existing glasses/prescription if available), "Can children get their eyes tested?" (Yes, from age 5+), "Is the eye exam covered by insurance?" (details with insurance partners), "What happens during a comprehensive eye exam?" (step-by-step explanation).

3. **Local & Promotional Content (20% of content):** Announcements about new brand arrivals, seasonal offers (Diwali spectacle sale, back-to-school packages), participation in local events, eye screening camp reports, and customer success stories. These drive immediate foot traffic and sales.

4. **Lifestyle & Fashion Content (15% of content):** Trend roundups (popular frame styles 2025), celebrity eyewear inspiration, matching glasses with outfits, and workplace eyewear (computer glasses, safety glasses). These appeal to fashion-conscious customers and younger demographics.

**Blog Page Design:**
The main blog listing page features a clean grid layout with blog post cards showing featured images, category tags (colour-coded), publish dates, titles, and brief excerpts. A realistic content calendar of 2 blog posts per month (24 posts/year) ensures consistent output without overwhelming the shop's capacity. Content can be written by a freelance health writer, generated using AI tools with expert review, or contributed by the optometrist themselves. Tamil-language versions of key articles should be prioritised for maximum local impact.

**SEO Impact:**
Each blog post must be optimised for specific long-tail keywords (e.g., "best sunglasses for driving in Tamil Nadu summer," "eye test cost in Sankarankovil," "progressive lenses vs bifocal which is better"). Proper meta titles, descriptions, header tags (H1, H2, H3), internal linking to product and booking pages, and image alt text are essential for every post.

## 5. Mobile-First Design Strategy

India is a mobile-first internet market — with over 750 million smartphone users and 85%+ of web traffic coming from mobile devices, designing for mobile is not merely a best practice but an existential requirement. In Tier-3 and Tier-4 towns like Sankarankovil, the dominance of mobile is even more pronounced, as many users skip desktops entirely and access the internet exclusively through smartphones — primarily low-to-mid-range Android devices from brands like Samsung, Xiaomi, Realme, and Vivo with screen sizes between 5.5 and 6.7 inches.

**Design Philosophy:**
The website will be built using a mobile-first responsive design methodology. This means the design process starts with the mobile layout as the baseline, and progressive enhancement adds complexity for larger screens (tablet, desktop). Every design decision — from typography and button sizes to navigation patterns and image selection — will be evaluated first on a 375px-wide mobile viewport before being adapted for wider screens.

**Typography & Touch Targets:**
Body text will be set at a minimum of 16px (1rem) with generous line-height (1.5–1.6) for comfortable reading on small screens. Headings will use a clear hierarchy (H1: 32px, H2: 24px, H3: 20px) with adequate spacing between sections. All interactive elements — buttons, links, form fields, navigation items — will have a minimum touch target of 48×48 pixels as per Google's Material Design guidelines, with adequate spacing (minimum 8px) between adjacent targets to prevent accidental taps.

**Navigation & Interaction Patterns:**
The mobile navigation will use a bottom tab bar for the three most critical actions (Home, Products, Book Exam) and a hamburger menu for secondary pages (About, Contact, Blog). Swiping gestures will be supported on carousels and product galleries. Infinite scrolling or "Load More" buttons (instead of pagination) will be used for product listings and blog posts. Sticky headers will collapse to a compact version with logo, search icon, and WhatsApp button as the user scrolls down.

**Performance Optimisation for Mobile:**
Given the variable internet speeds in Sankarankovil (4G speeds typically range from 5–25 Mbps, with occasional drops to 3G), aggressive performance optimisation is essential:

- **Image Compression:** All images will be served in WebP format with JPEG fallback, compressed to under 100KB for product thumbnails and under 300KB for hero images. Responsive images (srcset) will serve appropriately sized files based on device viewport.
- **Lazy Loading:** Images below the fold will load only as the user scrolls near them, reducing initial page weight significantly.
- **Critical CSS Inlining:** Above-the-fold CSS will be inlined in the HTML head to eliminate render-blocking resources, with the full stylesheet loaded asynchronously.
- **Minified Assets:** HTML, CSS, and JavaScript files will be minified and combined to reduce HTTP requests and file sizes.
- **CDN Delivery:** Static assets will be served through a Content Delivery Network (CloudFlare free tier) with the nearest edge node in Chennai or Bangalore, ensuring fast delivery to Sankarankovil users.
- **Target Performance Budget:** Under 1.5MB total page weight, under 3-second First Contentful Paint on a simulated 4G connection with a mid-range phone (Moto G Power equivalent).

**Offline Considerations:**
A lightweight service worker will cache key assets (logo, CSS, fonts, offline page) so that returning visitors see an immediate branded experience even on slow connections. The offline page will display contact information and a "Call Now" button, ensuring customers can always reach the shop.

## 6. Online Appointment Booking System

The online appointment booking system is the primary lead-generation mechanism for Sankaran Kovil Opticals, converting website visitors into in-store customers. A well-designed booking system reduces phone call volume, eliminates scheduling conflicts, and provides a modern, convenient experience that customers increasingly expect — even from local businesses.

**System Architecture:**
The booking system will be implemented as a combination of a frontend booking form (embedded on the Eye Exam Booking page) and a backend management dashboard accessible to the shop owner/staff. The system can be built using one of three approaches, depending on the chosen tech stack:

1. **Third-Party Booking Service (Recommended for Phase 1):** Integrate a dedicated appointment scheduling platform such as Cal.com, Setmore, or Fresha — all of which offer free tiers sufficient for a single-location business. These services provide the booking form widget, calendar management, automated reminders, and WhatsApp/email notifications out of the box. Integration is as simple as embedding an iframe or JavaScript widget on the booking page.

2. **WhatsApp Business API Integration:** Use a WhatsApp Business API provider like Interakt, Wappbiz, or Picky Assist (all India-focused) to create a chatbot-based booking flow. Customers click "Book Appointment" on the website, which opens WhatsApp with a pre-configured conversation flow: Step 1 — Select appointment type, Step 2 — Choose date, Step 3 — Choose time slot, Step 4 — Confirm details. This approach is extremely familiar to Indian users and has the highest completion rate.

3. **Custom-Built System:** For maximum control and branding, a custom booking system can be built using WordPress plugins (Amelia, Bookly) or custom code (Next.js + Supabase/Firebase). This provides a fully branded experience but requires more development effort and ongoing maintenance.

**Booking Flow (User Journey):**
1. User lands on the Eye Exam Booking page via homepage CTA, Google search, or WhatsApp link.
2. User selects the type of eye examination from visual cards (Routine, Comprehensive, Contact Lens, Children's, Diabetic Screening).
3. A dynamic calendar displays available dates (next 14 days); unavailable dates are greyed out.
4. User selects a preferred date; available time slots for that date appear.
5. User selects a time slot and fills in their name, phone number, and email (optional).
6. User reviews the booking summary (type, date, time, estimated cost) and confirms.
7. Confirmation screen appears with appointment details, a Google Maps link, and "Add to Calendar" buttons (Google Calendar, Apple Calendar).
8. Automatic WhatsApp/SMS confirmation sent to the customer with appointment details.
9. Shop staff receive a real-time notification (via WhatsApp group or dashboard alert).
10. Automated reminder sent to the customer 24 hours before the appointment.
11. After the appointment, a follow-up message asks for feedback and a Google review.

**Admin Dashboard Features:**
- Daily/weekly/monthly calendar view of all appointments
- Ability to block time slots for breaks, holidays, or off-site visits
- Customer history (repeat visits, past prescriptions, purchased products)
- Cancellation and rescheduling management

## 7. Virtual Try-On Feature — Feasibility Analysis

Virtual try-on (VTO) technology allows customers to preview how eyewear frames look on their face using their device's camera — either through augmented reality (AR) live view or by uploading a static photograph. Research into leading solutions (Fittingbox, Banuba, Zenni Optical, Warby Parker, Walmart AR) reveals that VTO is a proven technology that significantly reduces return rates in online eyewear retail and increases conversion rates by 30–50%. However, its feasibility for Sankaran Kovil Opticals must be evaluated across multiple dimensions.

**Technology Options & Providers:**

1. **Fittingbox Virtual Try-On:** The global leader in AR try-on for eyewear, used by over 500 optical retailers worldwide. Uses webcam-based AR to overlay 3D frame models on the user's face in real-time. Pricing starts at approximately ₹15,000–₹30,000/month for small businesses, which may be prohibitive for a single-location shop.

2. **Banuba AR SDK:** A mobile-first AR face tracking SDK that can be integrated into both web and mobile applications. Offers a try-on module specifically designed for eyewear. Pricing is custom (typically ₹50,000–₹2,00,000+ for development and licensing), making it more suitable for larger operations.

3. **Virtry / Ditto (Web-based):** Lightweight web widgets that use a photo upload approach rather than live AR. Customers upload a selfie, and the system maps frame models onto the photo. More affordable (₹5,000–₹15,000/month) and easier to integrate.

4. **Free/Open-Source Options:** Open-source face detection libraries (MediaPipe, Face-api.js) combined with 3D frame models can create a basic try-on experience. However, this requires significant custom development (₹1,00,000–₹3,00,000) and ongoing maintenance.

**Feasibility Assessment:**

| Criterion | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Cost** | Medium to High — Monthly SaaS subscriptions range from ₹5,000–₹30,000; custom development is ₹1L–₹3L+ |
| **Technical Complexity** | Medium — SaaS widgets are easy to embed; custom solutions require skilled developers |
| **User Readiness** | Medium — Younger customers (18–35) are comfortable with AR; older demographics may prefer in-person fitting |
| **Infrastructure** | Adequate — Most smartphones in Sankarankovil have front cameras capable of supporting AR |
| **ROI Potential** | Medium — Primarily a brand-differentiation feature for a physical store; less direct revenue impact |
| **Content Requirements** | High — Each frame needs a 3D model or multiple 2D images for the system to render accurately |

**Recommended Approach — Phased Implementation:**

**Phase 1 (Months 1–6): Skip VTO, Focus on Foundations.** Invest the budget in building a solid website, booking system, and WhatsApp integration. Use high-quality on-model photography and detailed frame descriptions to help customers make decisions online.

**Phase 2 (Months 6–12): Photo Upload Try-On.** Integrate an affordable photo-upload based VTO widget (e.g., Virtry) that allows customers to upload their selfie and see how select frames look. Limit this to the top 20–30 best-selling frames to keep content creation manageable. Cost: approximately ₹5,000–₹10,000/month.

**Phase 3 (12+ Months): AR Live Try-On.** If Phase 2 proves popular and drives measurable in-store visits, upgrade to a live AR try-on solution. Negotiate pricing with Fittingbox or an Indian AR provider (e.g., KiXR). Consider a dedicated mobile app if the customer base justifies it.

**Alternative Low-Cost Approach:**
Instead of full VTO, offer a "Face Shape Guide" — a simple interactive quiz where customers answer questions about their face shape, skin tone, and style preferences, and receive personalised frame recommendations from the shop's inventory. This costs nothing to implement and provides a similar decision-support function.

While not a traditional social media platform, Google Business Profile (GBP) is the most important digital presence for a local business. Optimisation includes:
- Complete and accurate business information (name, address, phone, hours, website URL, categories)
- High-quality photos (exterior, interior, team, products) — updated monthly
- Regular posts (weekly) with offers, events, and eye health tips
- Prompt response to all reviews (positive and negative)
- Q&A section actively maintained with common customer questions
- Products and services listed with accurate categories

### **Website Integration Mechanisms:**

1. **Social Media Feed Widget:** Embed an Instagram feed on the website's home page and footer, showing the latest 6–12 posts. This provides dynamic, fresh content on the website without manual updates and encourages visitors to follow the shop's social profiles.

2. **Social Sharing Buttons:** Every blog post, product page, and testimonial page includes sharing buttons for WhatsApp, Instagram (share to Stories), Facebook, Twitter/X, and LinkedIn. WhatsApp sharing is especially important — a single share can reach the sender's entire contact list.

3. **Social Proof Aggregation:** Automatically pull Google reviews and Instagram mentions to display on the website's testimonial section. Tools like Elfsight or Trustindex offer affordable widgets for this purpose.

4. **Unified Branding:** Consistent brand colours, logo, tone of voice, and visual style across all platforms and the website. Use the same profile photos, cover images, and bio descriptions everywhere.

5. **Cross-Promotion Strategy:**
   - Website → Social: "Follow us on Instagram for daily frame inspiration" banners
   - Social → Website: "Book your eye exam at sankarankovilopticals.com" in every Instagram bio and Facebook post
   - WhatsApp → Website: Share website links in WhatsApp broadcast messages
   - Blog → Social: Automatically share new blog posts on all social platforms
