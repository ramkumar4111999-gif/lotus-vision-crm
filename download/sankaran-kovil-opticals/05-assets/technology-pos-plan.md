## 3. Optical-Specific Software Features

### 3.1 Prescription Management

Prescription management is the backbone of any optical shop's software. A robust prescription module must store, retrieve, and manage patient vision data accurately and efficiently. The system should capture the complete prescription for each eye, including Sphere (SPH) power from -20.00D to +20.00D, Cylinder (CYL) power, Axis (0-180 degrees), Near Addition (ADD) for reading glasses, Prism values if any, Pupillary Distance (PD) measurement, and Interpupillary Distance (IPD). It should also record the optometrist's name, date of examination, and any clinical notes such as "patient reports headaches" or "advised anti-reflective coating."

**Key Prescription Features Required:**

- **Historical Prescription Tracking:** Every customer's prescriptions over time should be stored and accessible. This allows the optician to see how a customer's vision has changed — a 45-year-old who started with -1.00D myopia at age 20 may now have progressed to -3.50D, and this history helps in clinical advice. OptoSoft provides this feature natively.
- **Prescription Expiry Alerts:** The system should automatically flag prescriptions older than 6-12 months, prompting staff to recommend an eye re-examination when a returning customer wants to order new glasses with an old prescription. This is both clinically responsible and generates revenue from eye testing fees.
- **Digital Prescription Export:** The ability to export or share prescriptions via WhatsApp as a PDF or image. This is extremely valuable in Sankarankovil where many customers travel to Tirunelveli or Madurai for specialist consultations and may need to share their prescription.
- **Multi-Prescription Support:** Some customers need separate prescriptions for distance, reading, and computer use. The system should handle multiple active prescriptions per customer.
- **Prescription-to-Lens Mapping:** The software should automatically calculate lens pricing based on the prescription. For example, a -8.00D power may require high-index lenses (1.67 or 1.74 index) which cost more than standard CR-39 lenses for lower powers. The system should prompt the staff with lens material recommendations based on the prescription.
- **Contact Lens Prescription Tracking:** Separate module for contact lens prescriptions, including base curve, diameter, brand, and replacement schedule (daily/monthly/yearly disposables).

### 3.2 Inventory Tracking

Optical inventory is more complex than standard retail because of the unique nature of eyewear products. Frame inventory must track brand, model number, color, size, material (acetate, metal, titanium, TR-90), frame type (full-rim, half-rim, rimless, sunglasses), gender suitability, and price tier. Lens inventory must track by lens type (single vision, bifocal, progressive), material (CR-39, polycarbonate, high-index 1.67, high-index 1.74), power range (SPH and CYL in stock), coating options (anti-reflective, blue-cut, scratch-resistant, UV protection), and brand (Essilor, Zeiss, Crizal, Kodak, Rodenstock, and Indian brands like Vision Rx Lab, Sankara Nethralaya lenses).

**Key Inventory Features Required:**

- **Frame Inventory with Photos:** Store frame photographs alongside inventory data so staff can quickly show customers available options on a tablet, especially useful when the display stock is limited. Barcode each frame for quick lookup.
- **Lens Inventory by Power Range:** Track how many lenses are in stock for each power range (e.g., SPH -1.00 to -1.50 in CYL -0.50 to -0.75). Generate automatic reorder alerts when stock falls below minimum levels for common powers.
- **Minimum Stock Alerts:** Set threshold quantities for fast-moving items (e.g., Essilor Crizal Easy Pro in common powers) and receive alerts when reorder is needed. This prevents lost sales due to stockouts.
- **Supplier-Wise Inventory:** Track which supplier each batch came from, making it easy to return defective lenses or claim warranty.
- **Dead Stock Identification:** Reports that highlight frames or lenses that haven't sold in 6-12 months, enabling clearance sales to free up capital.
- **Multi-Location Inventory:** If a second branch opens in Tenkasi or Tirunelveli, the software should track inventory across locations and enable stock transfers.
- **Inventory Valuation Report:** Real-time view of total inventory value at cost price and MRP, essential for GST filing and financial planning.
- **Stock Audit Feature:** Periodic stock verification tool to reconcile physical stock with software records, identify theft/shrinkage, and correct discrepancies.

### 3.3 Customer Database

A comprehensive customer database (CRM) transforms an optical shop from a transactional retailer into a relationship-driven healthcare provider. The customer database should capture:

- **Basic Information:** Name (in English and optionally Tamil), phone number (primary and alternate), email address, date of birth, anniversary date, occupation, address.
- **Vision Profile:** Complete prescription history, date of first visit, date of last eye test, preferred lens brand, preferred frame brands, any allergies (e.g., nickel allergy for metal frames).
- **Purchase History:** Every transaction linked to the customer — frames purchased, lenses ordered, accessories bought, eye testing fees paid, insurance claims processed.
- **Family Linking:** Link family members together (husband-wife, parent-child) so that when one family member visits, the staff can see the entire family's eye care history. This is particularly valuable in Indian family dynamics where one person often buys glasses for multiple family members.
- **Loyalty Points:** Built-in loyalty program that awards points for purchases (e.g., 1 point per ₹100 spent) with redemption options (discount on next purchase, free lens cleaning kit, free frame).
- **Customer Segmentation:** Automatic categorization of customers (new, regular, premium, dormant) based on purchase frequency and value, enabling targeted marketing campaigns.
- **SMS/WhatsApp Integration:** One-click messaging to customers for appointment reminders, prescription readiness notifications, birthday/anniversary greetings, and promotional offers.
- **GDPR/Privacy Compliance:** Secure storage of personal health data with role-based access controls, ensuring only authorized staff can view prescription information.

### 3.4 Billing & Invoicing

Billing for an optical shop involves unique complexities beyond standard retail. A single customer transaction may include an eye testing fee (₹100-300), a frame (₹500-15,000), prescription lenses (₹300-8,000), lens coating add-ons (₹200-2,000), and accessories (cleaning solution ₹100, spectacle case ₹50). The billing system must handle all of these on a single invoice while applying correct GST rates:

- **Eyeframes and Sunglasses:** 18% GST (HSN Code 9003)
- **Prescription Lenses:** 18% GST (HSN Code 9001)
- **Contact Lenses:** 18% GST (HSN Code 9001)
- **Eye Testing Service:** 18% GST (if charged separately) or included in composite
- **Accessories (solutions, cases, chains):** 18% GST

**Key Billing Features Required:**

- **Professional Invoice Format:** Clean, branded invoice with the shop's name, address, GSTIN, phone number, and logo. Space for prescription details on the invoice (helpful when customers need to submit bills for insurance claims or employer reimbursement).
- **Estimate/Proforma Invoice:** Generate estimates before ordering custom lenses. The estimate can be converted to a final invoice when the customer picks up the glasses.
- **Advance Payment Tracking:** Record advance deposits (common when ordering expensive progressive lenses from Essilor/Zeiss labs) and show balance due on the final invoice.
- **Job Order Tracking:** For lenses that need to be sent to a lab for fitting (edging and mounting), generate a job order number that tracks the work-in-progress. The customer gets a job slip with estimated delivery date.
- **GST-Compliant Reports:** Auto-generation of GSTR-1, GSTR-3B summaries, and HSN-wise sales reports for monthly/quarterly GST filing.
- **Discount Management:** Flexible discount structure — percentage discounts, flat amount discounts, loyalty point redemptions, festival offers. Discount permissions can be restricted to the owner/manager.
- **Return & Exchange:** Handle returns within warranty period, exchanges for different frames, and credit note generation.
- **Daily/Monthly Sales Reports:** Real-time dashboards showing today's sales, this week's sales, this month's sales, compared to the same period last month/year.

### 3.5 Sales Reports & Analytics

Data-driven decision-making is essential for profitability. The POS system should provide comprehensive sales analytics:

- **Revenue Reports:** Daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly revenue trends. Comparison with previous periods to identify growth patterns and seasonal variations (e.g., higher sales during school reopening season in June, festival season in October-November).
- **Product-wise Sales:** Which frame brands sell best? Which lens types are most popular? Which price segments generate the most revenue? This data directly informs purchasing decisions.
- **Margin Analysis:** Profit margin per product category. High-margin items (lenses with coatings, premium frames) vs. low-margin items (basic frames, accessories). Staff should be trained to upsell high-margin products.
- **Customer Analytics:** New vs. returning customer ratio. Average transaction value. Most valuable customers (top 20% who generate 80% of revenue). Dormant customers who haven't visited in 6+ months.
- **Inventory Turnover Report:** How quickly inventory is being sold. Identify slow-moving stock for clearance sales. This is critical for an optical shop where unsold inventory can sit for 2-3 years.
- **Optometrist Performance:** If multiple optometrists work at the shop, track how many eye tests each performs daily, the average prescription value (lens+frame sale per eye test), and customer satisfaction scores.
- **Payment Mode Analysis:** UPI vs. cash vs. card vs. credit sales breakdown. Helps in cash flow planning and negotiating with payment gateway providers.
- **Supplier Performance:** Which suppliers deliver fastest? Which have the lowest defect rate? Which offer the best credit terms? This helps in supplier negotiations.

## 5. Computerized Eye Testing Setup

### 5.1 The Modern Refraction Room

A computerized eye testing setup combines the auto refractometer with digital visual acuity testing and integrated data management. The recommended setup for Sankaran Kovil Opticals transforms the refraction room into a professional, efficient space:

**Room Requirements:**
- Minimum room size: 8 feet x 10 feet (for 6-meter chart distance using mirrors)
- Alternatively, use a 3-meter room with a mirror to achieve effective 6-meter distance
- Light-controlled environment with dimmable LED lighting
- Air conditioning recommended (comfort for patient and equipment longevity)
- Dedicated power outlet with voltage stabilizer/UPS for equipment protection

## 7. Digital Prescription Records

### 7.1 The Shift from Paper to Digital

Digital prescription records eliminate the risk of lost prescriptions (a common issue when patients misplace paper prescriptions), enable quick retrieval of past prescriptions, and allow seamless sharing via WhatsApp. The system creates a complete digital health record for each customer.

### 7.2 Digital Prescription Features

**What OptoSoft's Prescription Module Provides:**

- **Structured Data Entry:** Prescription fields for OD (right eye) and OS (left eye) — SPH, CYL, AXIS, ADD, PD. Also records: near vision addition, intermediate vision (for computer users), prism correction.
- **Prescription Templates:** Quick-fill templates for common prescriptions (e.g., "standard myopia -2.00D") to speed up data entry.
- **Digital Prescription Card:** Generate a professional-looking digital prescription card (PDF/image) that can be printed or shared via WhatsApp. This card includes the shop's branding, the optometrist's credentials, and the complete prescription.
- **Prescription History Timeline:** Visual timeline showing all prescriptions for a customer, with date stamps and comparison highlights showing how the prescription has changed over time.
- **Auto-Refraction Import:** Direct import of data from the auto refractometer (Nidek, Topcon, etc.) via USB connection — eliminates manual data entry errors.
- **Prescription Validation:** System checks for unusual prescription values (e.g., -15.00D SPH for a new patient without history) and flags for verification, reducing the risk of dispensing errors.
- **Lens Recommendation Engine:** Based on the prescription, the system suggests appropriate lens types (e.g., "high index 1.67 recommended for SPH > -6.00D," "progressive recommended for ADD > +1.50D in patients > 40 years").
- **Prescription Sharing with Labs:** Direct export of prescription data to lens lab partners (Essilor, Zeiss, local labs) via WhatsApp or email, speeding up the order processing time.

### 7.3 Regulatory & Compliance Considerations

- **Data Privacy:** Patient prescription data is health information. Store it securely with encrypted cloud backup. Inform customers that their data is stored digitally and obtain verbal consent.
- **Prescription Validity:** In India, an optometrist's prescription is typically valid for 6-12 months. The system should flag expired prescriptions when a customer wants to order new glasses with an old prescription.
- **Optometrist Registration:** Ensure the practicing optometrist is registered with the Tamil Nadu Optometrists and Opticians Registration Board (TNOOR) and their registration number is printed on all prescriptions.
- **Record Retention:** Maintain digital prescription records for a minimum of 3 years (as per general medical record guidelines in India).
