## 1. Executive Summary

Sankaran Kovil Opticals is a newly established optical retail store located in the heart of Sankarankovil, a bustling town in Tenkasi District, Tamil Nadu. The town serves as a commercial hub for surrounding villages including Sivagiri, Kadayanallur, Puliyangudi, and Vasudevanallur, with a combined catchment population of approximately 3–4 lakh people. This plan outlines a comprehensive human resources and operations framework designed to build a professional, customer-centric team while ensuring full compliance with all applicable Tamil Nadu and Central Government regulations.

The staffing plan accounts for a 6-day operating week (closed on Tuesday, a standard practice for optical shops in Tamil Nadu) with operating hours of 9:00 AM to 8:30 PM (11.5 hours). A total team of **5 full-time employees** is proposed, with one additional part-time helper during peak hours and festival seasons. The estimated monthly payroll expenditure is approximately **₹1,10,000–₹1,25,000** including statutory contributions (EPF, ESI, bonus provisions). This plan prioritizes hiring locally from Sankarankovil and nearby towns to reduce attrition and build community trust, while ensuring all clinical roles are filled by qualified professionals as mandated by law.

Key operational priorities include: (a) establishing a systematic daily workflow with documented checklists, (b) creating a 4-week structured training program for all new hires, (c) implementing monthly performance reviews tied to customer satisfaction metrics, and (d) maintaining full regulatory compliance across seven critical licensing and registration requirements. The plan is designed to be scalable — if the business expands to a second location or adds online sales, the staffing and operational framework can be duplicated with minimal modifications.

## 3. Detailed Job Descriptions

### 3.1 Store Manager

**Role Overview:** The Store Manager is the operational backbone of Sankaran Kovil Opticals, responsible for the day-to-day running of the store, achieving sales targets, managing staff, and ensuring a superior customer experience. This individual reports directly to the Proprietor and is accountable for P&L management, inventory control, and regulatory compliance at the store level.

**Qualifications & Experience:**
- Minimum: Bachelor's degree (B.Com, BBA, or equivalent) from a recognized university
- Preferred: MBA or PG Diploma in Retail Management
- Experience: 3–5 years in retail management, preferably in optical retail, pharmacy, or healthcare retail
- Language: Fluency in Tamil (mandatory), basic English for reading prescriptions and product labels
- Age: 25–40 years preferred
- Must be computer-literate (basic billing software, MS Excel, WhatsApp Business)

**Key Responsibilities:**
- **Sales & Revenue Management:** Achieve monthly revenue targets (initial target: ₹3–5 lakh/month), monitor daily sales reports, analyze product-wise performance, and implement promotional strategies during festival seasons (Diwali, Pongal, Ayudha Puja)
- **Inventory Management:** Track stock levels using billing software, place orders with suppliers (Essilor, Zeiss, Crizal, Ray-Ban, Titan Eye+, etc.), manage the monthly reconciliation of physical stock vs. system stock, and minimize dead stock (frames older than 180 days)
- **Staff Supervision:** Create weekly duty rosters, approve leave requests, resolve inter-staff conflicts, conduct daily morning briefings (10 minutes), and ensure adherence to grooming standards (clean uniform, name badges, neat appearance)
- **Customer Relationship Management:** Handle escalated customer complaints, build a referral program, maintain a customer database with prescription history, and ensure follow-up calls for delivered spectacles (Day 2 and Day 7 post-delivery)
- **Financial Management:** Manage the daily cash box (opening balance ₹2,000), prepare daily cash reconciliation reports, handle bank deposits (2–3 times per week), and manage vendor payments
- **Compliance:** Ensure all licenses are displayed prominently, maintain statutory registers (attendance, leave, wages), file GST returns (GSTR-1 by 11th, GSTR-3B by 20th), and coordinate annual license renewals
- **Reporting:** Submit a weekly summary report to the Proprietor covering sales figures, footfall count, customer complaints, stock status, and any operational issues

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
1. Monthly revenue achievement (target: 100%)
2. Average bill value (target: ₹2,000–₹3,500)
3. Customer satisfaction score (target: 4.5/5.0 based on feedback forms)
4. Stock accuracy (target: >95% match between physical and system stock)
5. Staff attendance rate (target: >95%)

### 3.2 Optometrist

**Role Overview:** The Optometrist is the most technically skilled staff member, responsible for conducting comprehensive eye examinations, prescribing corrective lenses, fitting contact lenses, and providing professional eye care advice. In a Tier-3/4 town like Sankarankovil, the optometrist often serves as the primary eye care professional for the community, making this role critical to the store's reputation and clinical credibility.

**Qualifications & Experience:**
- **Mandatory:** Bachelor of Optometry (B.Optom) — 4-year degree from an institution approved by the Optometry Council of India (OCI) or equivalent
- **Alternative acceptable:** Diploma in Optometry (DOPT) — 2-year diploma from a recognized institution, provided the candidate has at least 2 years of clinical experience
- **Registration:** Must hold a valid registration with the Tamil Nadu Paramedical Council or the Optometry Council of India
- **Experience:** 0–3 years for freshers (with strong internship experience); 3–5 years preferred for independent practice
- **Language:** Tamil fluency essential; ability to explain prescriptions in simple Tamil to rural patients
- **Certifications (preferred):** Contact lens fitting certification, low vision aid certification

**Key Responsibilities:**
- **Eye Examinations:** Perform a minimum of 8–12 eye examinations per day, including refraction (retinoscopy, subjective refraction), visual acuity testing (Snellen chart at 6 meters and near vision at 40 cm), binocular vision assessment, color vision testing (Ishihara chart), and intraocular pressure screening (non-contact tonometry if equipment available)
- **Prescription Writing:** Issue accurate prescriptions following the standard format (SPH, CYL, AXIS, ADD, PD) with clear instructions for lens type recommendations (single vision, bifocal, progressive, photochromic)
- **Contact Lens Services:** Perform contact lens fitting for myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism (toric lenses), and presbyopia (multifocal lenses); teach insertion and removal techniques; advise on lens care solutions and replacement schedules (daily disposable, monthly, quarterly)
- **Product Recommendation:** Based on the patient's lifestyle, occupation, and prescription, recommend appropriate lens brands (Crizal Easy Pro, Essilor Eyezen, Zeiss Duravision, etc.) and lens coatings (anti-reflective, UV protection, blue light blocking)
- **Equipment Management:** Calibrate and maintain all ophthalmic equipment (auto refractometer, trial lens set, phoropter, slit lamp, lensometer); report any malfunction to the Store Manager immediately; maintain a daily equipment log
- **Clinical Record Keeping:** Maintain patient records in the clinic register and/or digital system (name, age, address, phone number, prescription history, follow-up dates); ensure patient confidentiality as per medical ethics standards
- **Referral System:** Identify patients requiring ophthalmological consultation (cataract suspects, glaucoma suspects, diabetic retinopathy cases, corneal ulcers) and refer them to the nearest eye hospital (Tenkasi Government Hospital, Aravind Eye Hospital — Tirunelveli, or private ophthalmologists in Sankarankovil)
- **Community Outreach:** Participate in free eye screening camps organized by the store (minimum 1 camp per quarter in nearby villages); conduct awareness sessions on eye health, screen time management for children, and diabetic eye care

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
1. Number of eye examinations conducted per day (target: 8–12)
2. Contact lens fitting conversions (target: 15–20% of eligible patients)
3. Prescription-to-sale conversion rate (target: >75% — i.e., at least 75% of patients who get an eye exam also purchase spectacles from the store)
4. Customer feedback score on clinical service (target: 4.7/5.0)
5. Equipment maintenance compliance (target: 100% — no equipment downtime due to negligence)

### 3.3 Senior Sales Associate

**Role Overview:** The Senior Sales Associate is the face of the store for most walk-in customers. This role requires a blend of product knowledge, interpersonal skills, and aesthetic sense to help customers choose the right frames and lenses. The Senior Sales Associate also mentors the Junior Sales Associate and manages the dispensing process from order placement to final delivery.

**Qualifications & Experience:**
- Minimum: Higher Secondary (12th pass) or Diploma in any discipline
- Preferred: Bachelor's degree, or certification in opticianry/dispensing from a recognized institute
- Experience: 2–4 years in optical retail sales or general retail sales (fashion retail experience is a plus)
- Language: Tamil fluency mandatory; basic English preferred
- Skills: Strong communication, color sense, face shape analysis ability, comfortable using billing software and basic computer operations
- Age: 22–35 years preferred

**Key Responsibilities:**
- **Frame Selection Assistance:** Greet every customer within 30 seconds of entry, understand their requirements (prescription type, lifestyle, budget, face shape), and recommend 4–6 suitable frame options from the displayed inventory. Demonstrate frames using the trial mirror station
- **Lens Consultation:** Based on the optometrist's prescription, explain lens options to the customer (single vision vs. bifocal vs. progressive, standard vs. premium brands) and help them choose within their budget. Upsell lens coatings (anti-reflective: ₹500–₹2,000, blue cut: ₹300–₹1,500, UV protection: ₹200–₹800)
- **Order Processing:** Accurately enter orders into the billing/inventory software, record frame measurements (PD, seg height, pantoscopic tilt), specify lens requirements, and generate order confirmations. Double-check all entries before submission to the lab
- **Dispensing & Fitting:** When finished spectacles arrive from the lab, verify the prescription using a lensometer, check frame alignment, adjust nose pads and temple arms for comfortable fit, and demonstrate proper usage and care to the customer
- **Upselling & Cross-Selling:** Actively promote accessories (spectacle cases: ₹100–₹500, cleaning solutions: ₹150–₹350, repair kits, prescription sunglasses) to every customer. Target accessory attachment rate of 40%
- **Inventory Display Management:** Ensure all frames are displayed neatly and attractively, rotate displays every 4–6 weeks, tag all frames with prices and barcodes, identify slow-moving stock and propose promotional pricing
- **Customer Follow-up:** Make courtesy calls 2 days after delivery to check comfort and satisfaction; handle minor adjustments (nose pad replacement, screw tightening) at no additional charge
- **Mentorship:** Train and guide the Junior Sales Associate on product knowledge, sales techniques, and store procedures

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
1. Monthly sales target achievement (individual target: ₹1.5–2.5 lakh)
2. Average bill value (target: ₹2,500)
3. Accessory attachment rate (target: 40%)
4. Customer feedback score (target: 4.5/5.0)
5. Number of upselling conversions per week (target: 15–20)

- Skills: Basic computer literacy (able to operate billing software after training), good communication, polite and approachable demeanor

1. Billing accuracy (target: 100% — zero billing errors)
2. Customer greeting compliance (target: 100% of walk-ins greeted within 30 seconds)
3. Stock entry accuracy (target: >98% entries match physical stock)
4. Learning progress (target: pass internal product knowledge test with 80%+ score within 60 days)

### 4.2 Salary Breakdown & Notes

**DA / Other Allowances:** Includes dearness allowance, transport allowance (₹1,000/month), and mobile/internet allowance (₹500/month for Store Manager and Optometrist). These are fully taxable.

## 5. Staff Training Program

### 5.1 Training Philosophy & Approach

A well-trained staff is the single most important differentiator between Sankaran Kovil Opticals and competitors. In a Tier-3/4 town, many customers visit an optical shop with limited knowledge about lens technology, frame materials, or eye health. The staff's ability to educate, guide, and reassure customers directly impacts sales conversion, customer loyalty, and word-of-mouth referrals. The training program is designed as a **4-week intensive onboarding** for new hires, followed by **ongoing monthly refresher sessions** (minimum 4 hours/month per employee).

Training will be delivered through a combination of methods: (a) manufacturer-provided training modules (Essilor, Zeiss, and frame brand reps), (b) in-house training by the Store Manager and Proprietor, (c) video tutorials from the Optometry Council of India and retail training platforms, and (d) on-the-job shadowing and role-playing exercises. All training sessions must be documented with attendance records and completion certificates stored in the employee file.

## 7. Store Opening & Closing Procedures

### 7.1 Detailed Opening Procedure (8:30 AM – 9:00 AM)

**Step 1 — Security Check (8:30 AM)**
The Store Manager (or designated senior staff) arrives first and performs a perimeter check. Walk around the exterior of the shop, check for broken glass, tampered locks, or signs of forced entry. If anything is suspicious, do not enter — call the police (100) and inform the Proprietor immediately. If everything is normal, unlock the main door using the primary key.

**Step 2 — Alarm Deactivation & Infrastructure Startup (8:35 AM)**
Deactivate the alarm system using the authorized code. Switch on the electrical mains. Turn on all lights in sequence: entrance signage → main display area → consultation room → dispensing area → washroom. Turn on ceiling fans first, then AC units (set to 24°C). Check the water dispenser and replace the bottle if empty.

**Step 3 — Equipment Warm-Up (8:40 AM)**
The Optometrist switches on clinical equipment. Auto-refractometer needs a 10-minute warm-up for accurate readings. Slit lamp and lensometer should be powered on and checked with the calibration phantom. Any equipment showing error codes must be noted and the supplier contacted.

**Step 4 — Cleaning & Sanitization (8:40–8:55 AM)**
The Helper performs the cleaning checklist. Special attention must be given to the frame display area (fingerprints on glass cases are a common customer complaint), the trial mirror station, and the consultation room. The trial frames must be laid out neatly with the cleaning spray bottle and tissue paper nearby.

**Step 5 — Cash & System Check (8:50 AM)**
The Store Manager opens the cash box, counts the opening balance (₹2,000 in denominations: 10×₹100, 5×₹50, 5×₹20, 20×₹10, 25×₹5, 50×₹2 coins), and enters this in the daily cash register. The POS computer is booted, the billing software is launched, and yesterday's closing balance is verified against the sales report.

**Step 6 — Team Briefing (8:55 AM)**
The Store Manager conducts a 5-minute standing briefing at the counter. Topics covered: (a) yesterday's performance vs. target, (b) today's individual targets, (c) any special offers running, (d) expected VIP customers or deliveries, (e) any product or operational updates. End with an energetic team cheer or motivational statement.

**Step 7 — Open for Business (9:00 AM)**
The Helper opens the main shutter. The "OPEN" board is placed outside. The glass door is unlocked. Background music (soft instrumental/tamil melodies at low volume) is started if available. Staff take their positions: Optometrist in the consultation room, Senior Sales at the frame display, Junior Sales at the billing counter, Helper at the entrance.

### 7.2 Detailed Closing Procedure (8:00 PM – 8:30 PM)

**Step 1 — Last Customer Call (8:00 PM)**
The Helper or Junior Sales announces: "Customers, our store will be closing in 30 minutes. If you need an eye examination, we recommend visiting us tomorrow. You are welcome to browse our collection until 8:30 PM." At 8:15 PM, a second announcement: "Customers, we are closing in 15 minutes. We invite you to complete your purchases and visit us again."

**Step 2 — Final Sales & Billing (8:00–8:15 PM)**
The Senior Sales and Junior Sales complete all pending transactions. No new customers are turned away before 8:15 PM, but eye examinations stop at 7:30 PM to allow the Optometrist time for record-keeping and equipment shutdown. All walk-in customers after 8:15 PM are informed of closing time and invited to return the next day.

**Step 3 — Cash Reconciliation (8:15 PM)**
The Store Manager counts all cash in the register. The total is compared against the POS sales report. Breakdown: Cash sales total = Total cash in register − Opening balance (₹2,000). UPI total and card total are verified from the bank app/POS machine. Discrepancies of more than ₹50 must be investigated and documented. If cash is short, the staff member responsible for the shift must provide an explanation in writing.

**Step 4 — Dispensing & Inventory Check (8:15 PM)**
The Senior Sales checks all trial frames on the dispensing table and returns them to the display. Any finished spectacles ready for delivery are locked in the dispensing cabinet with a note of the customer's name and phone number. The lab delivery log is checked — were all expected deliveries received? Any pending orders past the promised delivery date are flagged for the Store Manager to follow up the next morning.

**Step 5 — Equipment Shutdown (8:20 PM)**
The Optometrist powers down all equipment in reverse order: slit lamp → lensometer → phoropter → auto-refractometer. Covers are placed over sensitive equipment. The daily equipment log is updated with "equipment working normally" or a note of any issues. The Optometrist reviews the next day's appointment register and prepares any required trial lenses.

**Step 6 — Cleaning & Shutdown (8:20–8:25 PM)**
The Helper performs a quick sweep and mop of the customer areas. The washroom is cleaned and sanitized. All lights in the consultation room, dispensing area, and storage are switched off. Only the security light (a single CFL/LED near the entrance) and the CCTV (if installed) remain on.

**Step 7 — Security & Locking (8:25 PM)**
The Store Manager ensures the cash bag (with bank deposit amount) is placed in the safe. The dispensing cabinet key and storage room key are placed in the key box. The alarm system is activated. The main shutter is pulled down and locked with the padlock. The glass door is locked from inside before the shutter is closed. A final visual check through the shutter gap confirms no lights are on and no one is inside.

**Step 8 — End-of-Day Report (8:30 PM)**

## 10. Leave & Attendance Policy

### 10.1 Leave Policy — As Per Tamil Nadu Shops & Establishments Act, 1947

Sankaran Kovil Opticals follows the Tamil Nadu Shops and Establishments Act, 1947, and the associated Rules of 1948. The Act mandates minimum leave entitlements, and our policy provides benefits at or above the statutory minimum to ensure employee welfare and retention.

**Weekly Rest Day:**
- All employees are entitled to 1 full rest day per week (Tuesday — store is closed)
- If an employee is required to work on the weekly rest day (e.g., during festival seasons), they shall be granted a compensatory rest day within the same week or the following week

**Earned Leave (EL) / Privilege Leave:**
- Entitlement: 1 day for every 20 days of work performed (approximately 15 days per year for a 6-day work week)
- Accumulation: Maximum accumulation of 24 days (as per Section 24 of the TN Shops & Establishments Act)
- Encashment: Unutilized earned leave may be encashed at the end of the calendar year or upon separation from service, at the rate of average daily wages
- Carry Forward: Up to 12 days of earned leave may be carried forward to the next calendar year; balance beyond 12 days is encashed or lapsed as per company policy

**Casual Leave (CL):**
- Entitlement: 12 days per calendar year (in addition to earned leave)
- Availment: Maximum 2 days at a time; cannot be combined with weekends or other holidays
- Non-accumulative: Unused casual leave lapses at the end of the calendar year
- Approval: Must be approved by the Store Manager at least 1 day in advance (except in emergencies)

**Sick Leave (SL) / Medical Leave:**
- Entitlement: 12 days per calendar year (company policy exceeds the statutory minimum)
- Documentation: For leave exceeding 2 consecutive days, a medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner is mandatory
- Advance Intimation: Employee must inform the Store Manager by 9:00 AM on the first day of sick leave
- Accumulation: Up to 6 days of unused sick leave may be carried forward; remaining lapses

**Festival & National Holidays:**
- Tamil Nadu has approximately 15 public holidays per year as declared by the Government of Tamil Nadu
- The store observes the following mandatory holidays (store closed):
  - Pongal (January 14–15)
  - Republic Day (January 26)
  - May Day (May 1)
  - Independence Day (August 15)
  - Vinayagar Chaturthi (1 day)
  - Ayudha Puja / Saraswathi Puja (1 day)
  - Diwali (2 days)
  - Christmas (December 25)
  - New Year's Day (January 1)
  - Any additional holidays as declared by the TN Government

**Maternity Leave:**
- As per the Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017: 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for the first two children (of which up to 8 weeks can be before the expected delivery date)
- For the third child: 12 weeks
- Applicable to: All female employees regardless of salary level (the company voluntarily extends this benefit)
- The employee must give written notice at least 8 weeks before the expected delivery date

**Special Leave:**
- Marriage: 3 days paid leave for the employee's own marriage; 1 day for a sibling's marriage
- Bereavement: 3 days paid leave for the death of an immediate family member (spouse, parent, child, sibling)
- Paternity Leave: 5 days paid leave (company policy, not mandated for shops) within 30 days of the child's birth

### 10.2 Attendance & Punctuality Policy

**Working Hours:**
- 8 hours per day, 48 hours per week (as per Tamil Nadu Shops & Establishments Act)
- Overtime: If an employee works beyond 48 hours in a week, overtime at the rate of 1.5 times the ordinary hourly wage is payable
- Overtime must be pre-approved by the Store Manager and documented

**Punctuality:**
- All staff must arrive at least 30 minutes before the store opening time (i.e., by 8:30 AM for a 9:00 AM opening)

### 11.2 Detailed Compliance Notes

**1. Shop & Establishment License (Tamil Nadu Shops & Establishments Act, 1947):**
This is the foundational license for operating any shop or commercial establishment in Tamil Nadu. The application is submitted to the Inspector of Shops for the Tenkasi District through the online portal (tnlabour.tn.gov.in) or at the local labour office. Required documents include: (a) filled application form, (b) copy of rent agreement or ownership proof, (c) NOC from landlord (if rented), (d) identity proof of proprietor (Aadhaar, PAN), (e) 2 passport-size photographs, (f) building plan/approval from the municipality. The license must be displayed prominently at the store entrance. The Act regulates working hours (max 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week for adults; max 6 hours/day for young persons aged 14–18), rest intervals (minimum 30 minutes after 5 hours of work), overtime (at 1.5x rate), weekly rest day, and leave entitlements. Penalties for non-compliance: ₹100–₹2,500 for hours/leave violations; ₹500–₹5,000 for operating without a license.

**2. GST Registration & Compliance:**
Sankaran Kovil Opticals must register under GST and obtain a GSTIN (GST Identification Number). As a retail optical shop, the applicable HSN codes and tax rates are:
- **9001:** Spectacle lenses (corrective lenses) — 12% GST
- **9002:** Contact lenses — 12% GST
- **9003:** Spectacle frames (mounted or unmounted) — 12% GST
- **9004:** Sunglasses (non-corrective) — 12% GST
- **9018:** Ophthalmic instruments and appliances — 12% GST

GST filing obligations:
- **GSTR-1:** Filed by the 11th of the following month — details of all outward supplies (sales)
- **GSTR-3B:** Filed by the 20th of the following month — summary of inward and outward supplies, tax liability, and payment
- **GSTR-9:** Annual return filed by December 31 — comprehensive summary of the financial year
- Input Tax Credit (ITC) can be claimed on purchases of frames, lenses, equipment, and consumables from GST-registered suppliers — this significantly reduces the effective tax burden. The shop must maintain proper tax invoices for all sales and purchases.

**3. MD-42 License (Medical Device Sales License):**
From April 1, 2020, the CDSCO requires all optical shops selling contact lenses to obtain an MD-42 license under the Medical Devices Rules, 2017. Contact lenses are classified as Class B (low-medium risk) medical devices. To obtain this license, the shop must:
- Apply online through the CDSCO portal (cdscoonline.gov.in) or through the Tamil Nadu State Drug Control Authority
- Provide proof of a qualified optometrist on staff (B.Optom or equivalent)
- Submit the shop layout plan (showing a separate dispensing area with hygienic conditions)
- Provide details of storage facilities (temperature-controlled area for certain contact lens products)
- Submit quality assurance documentation

The license is valid for 5 years and must be renewed 6 months before expiry. Selling contact lenses without an MD-42 license can result in seizure of stock, fines of up to ₹5,00,000, and imprisonment of up to 3 years.

**4. Trade License (Local Body):**
A trade license from the Sankarankovil Town Panchayat or Municipality is required for operating any commercial establishment within its jurisdiction. The application requires: (a) filled application form, (b) building plan approval, (c) NOC from the local body for commercial use, (d) fire safety NOC (if required by the local body), (e) sanitary NOC. This license typically costs ₹2,000–₹5,000 per year and must be renewed annually. The local body may also impose a professional tax on the establishment.

**5. EPF & ESI Compliance:**
Even though EPF is technically mandatory only for establishments with 20+ employees, Sankaran Kovil Opticals voluntarily registers for EPF to attract and retain quality staff (especially the optometrist). EPF registration is done through the EPFO unified portal (unifiedportal-epfo.in). The employer contributes 12% of the employee's basic salary + DA, and the employee contributes a matching 12% (of which 8.33% goes to the Employees' Pension Scheme and 3.67% to the EPF). Deposits are due by the 15th of the following month.

ESI registration is required for establishments with 10+ employees where employee salary is ≤ ₹21,000/month. The employer contributes 3.25% and the employee contributes 0.75% of gross salary. ESI provides comprehensive medical coverage (free treatment at ESI hospitals) and cash benefits (sickness, maternity, disability, dependents' benefit). Half-yearly contributions are due by May 11 and November 11.

## 12. Insurance Requirements

### 12.1 Mandatory Insurance

**1. Employees' State Insurance (ESI):**
While not strictly an "insurance policy" that needs to be purchased separately, ESI registration effectively provides the following coverages to all eligible employees (gross salary ≤ ₹21,000/month) at virtually no additional cost beyond the statutory contribution:

- **Full Medical Benefit:** Free outpatient and inpatient treatment at ESI dispensaries and hospitals for the employee and their dependents (spouse, children, dependent parents)
- **Sickness Benefit:** 70% of average daily wages for up to 91 days per year for certified illness
- **Maternity Benefit:** 100% of average daily wages for up to 26 weeks (for the first two children)
- **Disability Benefit:** 70% of average daily wages for temporary disability; 90% for permanent partial disability
- **Dependents' Benefit:** 70% of average daily wages paid to dependents in case of the employee's death due to employment injury

Nearby ESI hospitals for Sankarankovil residents include: ESI Hospital, Tirunelveli (55 km) and ESI Dispensary, Tenkasi (28 km).

### 12.4 Insurance Claim Process

In the event of a claim (fire, theft, accident, liability), the following procedure must be followed:

### 13.3 Grievance Redressal Mechanism

1. **Step 1 — Informal Resolution:** The employee discusses the issue directly with the Store Manager within 3 working days of the incident
2. **Step 2 — Written Complaint:** If unresolved, the employee submits a written complaint to the Store Manager (or directly to the Proprietor if the complaint is about the Store Manager)
3. **Step 3 — Investigation:** The Proprietor conducts an impartial investigation within 7 working days, including interviews with the concerned parties and any witnesses
4. **Step 4 — Resolution:** A decision is communicated in writing within 10 working days of receiving the complaint; if the employee is unsatisfied, they may appeal within 7 working days
5. **Step 5 — External Redressal:** If internal mechanisms fail, the employee may approach the Labour Commissioner, Tenkasi District, under the Industrial Disputes Act or the Tamil Nadu Shops & Establishments Act
