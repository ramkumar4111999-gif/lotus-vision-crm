## 1. Executive Summary

Sankaran Kovil Opticals is uniquely positioned in Sankarankovil, a growing temple town in Tirunelveli district, Tamil Nadu, with a population of approximately 70,000 and a significant floating population of pilgrims and tourists visiting the Arulmigu Sankaranarayana Swamy Temple. The Indian eyewear market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 11.4% from 2025 to 2030, with the domestic market size estimated at USD 4.1 billion in 2024. This growth is driven by increasing awareness of eye health, rising screen time across all age groups, and a shift from purely functional eyewear to fashion-conscious purchases.

This Customer Acquisition Plan outlines a multi-channel, 360-degree approach to bring new customers through our doors. Our strategy blends traditional offline marketing methods that resonate deeply in Tier-3 and Tier-4 towns like Sankarankovil — such as wall paintings, auto-rickshaw advertisements, temple event activations, and school screening programs — with modern digital tools like Google My Business optimisation, WhatsApp marketing, and social media presence. Each channel is designed to complement the others, creating a surround-sound brand presence that builds trust, awareness, and footfall.

Our 12-month target is to acquire 1,200 new customers through a combination of offline campaigns (40%), referral programs (20%), school and corporate camps (20%), and online channels (20%). The estimated Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) ranges from Rs. 50 to Rs. 200 per customer depending on the channel, with offline community events delivering the lowest CAC and digital advertising commanding the highest. The plan includes a detailed monthly budget allocation, specific action items for the sales team, and measurable KPIs to track progress.

A key differentiator for Sankaran Kovil Opticals will be our deep integration into the community fabric of Sankarankovil — leveraging the temple ecosystem, partnering with local educational institutions, and building a word-of-mouth referral engine that competitors cannot easily replicate. This is not just a marketing plan; it is a community integration strategy designed to make Sankaran Kovil Opticals the default choice for eyecare in Sankarankovil and the surrounding 30-kilometre radius covering areas like Puliangudi, Kadayanallur, Tenkasi, and Vasudevanallur.

## 2. Customer Acquisition Channels — Offline

### 2.1 Pamphlets & Handbills Distribution

Pamphlet distribution remains one of the most cost-effective customer acquisition methods for small-town businesses in India. In Sankarankovil, where literacy rates are high and people actively engage with printed materials distributed at public gathering points, this channel delivers strong recall. We will design a series of bilingual pamphlets (Tamil and English) that highlight our services — comprehensive eye examinations, branded frames, contact lenses, sunglasses, and repair services. Each pamphlet will include a tear-off coupon offering a 10% discount on the first purchase or a free eye test, enabling us to track response rates by distribution zone.

**Distribution Strategy:** Pamphlets will be distributed at high-traffic nodes including the Sankaranarayana Swamy Temple entrance, Sankarankovil bus stand, weekly vegetable markets (santhai), near schools and colleges, and at all local function halls during wedding seasons. We will target a distribution of 2,000 pamphlets per month during the first quarter, increasing to 5,000 per month during festival seasons (Aadi Thapasu in July, Panguni Uthiram in March). Each batch will carry a unique coupon code to measure conversion rates from each distribution point. We estimate a conversion rate of 1.5% to 3% from pamphlets, translating to 30 to 150 new walk-ins per month.

**Design Guidelines:** Pamphlets must be printed on quality 130 GSM paper with vibrant colours. The front side will feature the shop name, a photograph of the store exterior, services offered, and a limited-period offer. The back side will include a simple eye health tips section in Tamil, customer testimonials, and a map showing the shop location. This educational approach adds perceived value, reducing the likelihood that the pamphlet is discarded immediately.

### 2.2 Banners & Hoardings

Strategically placed banners and hoardings provide continuous brand visibility in a town where commuters follow predictable daily routes. We will deploy a mix of permanent and temporary banners across Sankarankovil. Two permanent flex hoardings (10x20 feet) will be erected on the main road — one near the temple junction and one near the bus stand — featuring our brand identity, a compelling tagline in Tamil ("Kannukkul Mangalyam — Sankaran Kovil Opticals"), and a picture of a happy family wearing eyeglasses. These will be illuminated with LED strip lights for night visibility.

Temporary banners (3x6 feet) will be put up during specific campaigns — school admission season (April-May), Aadi Thapasu festival (July), Diwali season (October-November), and Pongal (January). These banners will highlight seasonal offers like "Back-to-School Eye Check: Free for Students" or "Diwali Dhamaka: 20% Off on Sunglasses." Banners will be placed at 15 key locations including the Tenkasi road junction, Rajapalayam road, Kadayanallur road, Puliangudi junction, and at all entry/exit points of the town. Each banner placement costs approximately Rs. 3,000 to Rs. 8,000 per month depending on size and location, and we will allocate Rs. 25,000 per month for this channel.

### 2.3 Wall Paintings (Wall Art Advertising)

Wall paintings are an iconic and deeply trusted advertising medium in Tamil Nadu's smaller towns. Unlike flex banners that are seen as temporary commercial messaging, wall paintings convey permanence, reliability, and community presence. We will commission hand-painted wall advertisements on compound walls facing main roads at 10 strategic locations throughout Sankarankovil and on the highways leading to the town from Tenkasi, Kadayanallur, and Rajapalayam directions. Each wall painting will measure approximately 8x12 feet and feature our logo, a Tamil tagline, key services (eye testing, frames, lenses, sunglasses, contact lenses), and the shop address with a simple directional arrow.

**Locations for Wall Paintings:** Priority locations include walls near the Sankarankovil railway station (for passengers arriving from Tirunelveli and Madurai), walls near the KSRTC bus stand, walls along the Tenkasi-Sankarankovil main road (NH 744 area), walls near major junctions like the Taluk Office road and Market road, and compound walls of partner businesses (schools, clinics, tea stalls) who agree to host our wall painting in exchange for a reciprocal arrangement. Each wall painting costs approximately Rs. 4,000 to Rs. 6,000 and lasts 2 to 3 years, making this the most cost-effective long-term branding channel with a monthly amortised cost of under Rs. 200 per location. We will paint 10 walls in Year 1.

### 2.4 Auto-Rickshaw Advertisements

Auto-rickshaws are the primary mode of last-mile public transport in Sankarankovil, and they travel through every neighbourhood, village access road, and commercial area. Advertising on auto-rickshards provides mobile, multi-location visibility at a fraction of the cost of traditional hoardings. We will partner with 15 to 20 auto-rickshaw drivers in Sankarankovil to display our advertisements on the rear panel of their vehicles. The advertisement will be a high-quality vinyl sticker (2x3 feet) featuring our logo, a catchy Tamil tagline ("Neenga Solla, Naanga Solra Sankaran Kovil Opticals"), phone number, and a QR code linking to our Google Maps location.

**Driver Partnership Model:** Rather than paying a flat monthly fee, we will offer auto drivers a referral commission of Rs. 50 for every new customer who visits our shop and mentions the driver's auto number. This creates a motivated sales network of 20 micro-influencers who will organically recommend our shop to passengers. The initial vinyl wrap costs Rs. 500 per auto, with a total Year 1 investment of Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 15,000 for 20 autos. We will also provide drivers with our pamphlets to distribute to passengers. Given that each auto serves 50 to 80 passengers per day, our 20 autos generate an estimated 30,000 to 48,000 impressions per month.

### 2.5 Local Events & Sponsorships

Participating in and sponsoring local events is a powerful community integration strategy for Sankarankovil. We will actively participate in the annual Aadi Thapasu festival (held in July/Aadi month at the Sankaranarayana Swamy Temple), which attracts thousands of devotees from across Tamil Nadu and Kerala over 10 days. During this festival, we will set up a temporary eye checkup booth near the temple, distribute free vision screening cards, and offer pilgrims a special "Temple Visit Combo" — free eye test + 15% off on frames. This leverages the high footfall while associating our brand with a deeply cultural and spiritual event.

Beyond the temple festival, we will sponsor local events including school annual day functions, college cultural fests (at Government Arts College, Sankarankovil), local cricket tournaments, and blood donation camps. Each sponsorship includes branding (banners at the venue), distribution of our promotional materials, and a brief on-stage mention. We will allocate Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 15,000 per event sponsorship, with a total annual events budget of Rs. 60,000. Sponsorship of local events builds goodwill and positions Sankaran Kovil Opticals as a community-oriented business rather than just a commercial establishment, which is critical in the trust-based purchasing culture of small-town Tamil Nadu.

### 2.6 Newspaper & Cable TV Advertising

For broader reach within Sankarankovil and surrounding areas, we will leverage local newspapers and cable television. A quarter-page advertisement in a local Tamil daily (such as Dinamalar or Dinathanthi Tirunelveli edition) costs approximately Rs. 3,000 to Rs. 5,000 and reaches an estimated 15,000 to 20,000 readers in the Sankarankovil-Tenkasi region. We will run newspaper ads once a month during the first 6 months, increasing to twice a month during festival seasons.

Cable TV is the dominant entertainment medium in Sankarankovil homes, and local cable operators offer scrolling text advertisements and video advertisement slots at very affordable rates — Rs. 500 to Rs. 2,000 per day for a 10-second video spot. We will produce a 30-second video advertisement in Tamil featuring our optometrist, store interiors, and customer testimonials, and run it during prime time (6 PM to 9 PM) for 5 days each month. The cable TV channel will also create a scrolling text advertisement ("Sankaran Kovil Opticals — Free Eye Checkup This Week — Near Temple Junction") that runs daily throughout the month for Rs. 1,500.

## 3. Customer Acquisition Channels — Online

### 3.1 Google My Business (GMB) Optimisation

Google My Business is the single most important digital tool for a local business in India. When someone in Sankarankovil or nearby towns searches for "optical shop near me" or "eye hospital Sankarankovil" on Google, our GMB listing must appear in the top 3 results on the map pack. We will create and fully optimise our GMB profile with the following elements: accurate business name (Sankaran Kovil Opticals), complete address with pincode (627756), phone number, WhatsApp number, website (if available), business hours, high-quality exterior and interior photographs (minimum 20 photos), a list of all services with descriptions, accepted insurance details, and product categories.

**Review Management:** We will actively solicit reviews from every satisfied customer. After each purchase, our staff will guide the customer to leave a Google review, offering a small incentive such as a free lens cleaning kit or a discount on their next purchase. Our target is to accumulate 100+ five-star reviews within 12 months, which will significantly boost our local search ranking. We will respond to every review — positive and negative — within 24 hours, demonstrating active engagement. GMB posts (similar to social media posts) will be published weekly, featuring offers, eye health tips, new arrivals, and customer stories. We will also use the GMB Q&A feature to pre-populate answers to common questions about pricing, insurance, and appointment requirements.

### 3.2 Social Media Marketing — Facebook & Instagram

Facebook is the dominant social media platform in Tier-3 and Tier-4 Tamil Nadu, with high penetration among adults aged 25 to 55 — our primary customer demographic for prescription eyewear. Instagram is gaining traction among younger audiences (18 to 30) and is essential for showcasing fashionable frames and sunglasses. We will maintain active profiles on both platforms with a content calendar featuring:

- **Frequency:** 4 posts per week on Facebook, 3 posts per week on Instagram
- **Content Mix:** 40% eye health education (in Tamil), 30% product showcases (new frames, sunglasses collections, contact lenses), 20% customer stories and before/after photos (with consent), 10% promotional offers and event announcements
- **Local Hashtags:** #Sankarankovil #Tenkasi #Tirunelveli #SankaranKovilOpticals #EyeCareTamilNadu #KannukkulMangalyam
- **Paid Advertising:** Facebook and Instagram ads targeted to users within a 30-km radius of Sankarankovil, aged 18 to 60, interested in health, fashion, or eyewear. Budget: Rs. 3,000 per month (Rs. 100/day), with increased spending during festivals (Rs. 5,000/month during Aadi Thapasu and Diwali).
- **Video Content:** Short Reels (15 to 30 seconds) showing frame fitting, eye exam process, customer reactions, and behind-the-scenes content. Video content receives 2-3x more engagement than static posts.

### 3.3 WhatsApp Marketing

WhatsApp is the primary digital communication tool in India, with over 500 million users. For a small-town business like ours, WhatsApp is not just a marketing channel — it is the customer relationship backbone. We will implement a comprehensive WhatsApp strategy in three phases:

**Phase 1 — WhatsApp Business Profile Setup:** Register for WhatsApp Business with a verified business profile including our logo, business description, address, product catalogue (photos of frames, sunglasses, lenses), and quick reply templates for common queries (pricing, appointment booking, insurance questions). Use automated greeting messages ("Welcome to Sankaran Kovil Opticals! How can we help you today?"), away messages, and labels to organise contacts.

**Phase 2 — Broadcast Lists & Status Updates:** Build segmented broadcast lists (existing customers, new leads, school contacts, corporate contacts) and send weekly broadcast messages with eye health tips, new product announcements, and exclusive offers. Use WhatsApp Status daily (morning greeting, eye health fact of the day, behind-the-scenes content, customer testimonials). WhatsApp Status has a 90%+ view rate among contacts, making it the highest-engagement content format available.

**Phase 3 — WhatsApp Communities:** Create a "Sankaran Kovil Opticals Eye Health Community" open group where members receive free eye care tips, can ask questions, and get early access to offers. This builds a loyal digital community that amplifies word-of-mouth. We will also create a separate broadcast list for referring doctors (ophthalmologists and general physicians) to keep them updated on our services and facilitate referrals.

### 3.4 YouTube Channel

YouTube is the second-largest search engine in India and is particularly powerful in Tamil Nadu, where video content consumption is extremely high. We will create a YouTube channel for Sankaran Kovil Opticals featuring: (a) Tamil-language educational videos on eye health topics (e.g., "How to protect your eyes from mobile screen damage", "Signs your child needs glasses", "Difference between bifocal and progressive lenses"), (b) virtual shop tour and frame collection walkthroughs, (c) customer testimonial videos, and (d) coverage of our community events like eye screening camps. We will aim for one video per month, each 3 to 5 minutes long. While YouTube is a long-term investment, even modest local viewership (100-500 views per video) builds credibility and supports our Google search ranking through video SEO.

### 3.5 Google Ads (Search & Display)

For immediate visibility when people actively search for optical services, we will run Google Ads campaigns targeting search queries like "optical shop Sankarankovil," "eye test near me," "spectacles shop Tenkasi road," "best optician in Sankarankovil," and "sunglasses shop near me." We will use a combination of Search Ads (text ads appearing at the top of Google search results) and Display Ads (banner ads on relevant websites). Budget allocation: Rs. 5,000 per month for Google Ads, with a target Cost Per Click (CPC) of Rs. 5 to Rs. 15. With an estimated 300 to 600 clicks per month and a 5% to 8% conversion rate from click to store visit, Google Ads can deliver 15 to 48 new customers per month. We will use location targeting (15-km radius), language targeting (Tamil and English), and ad scheduling (9 AM to 9 PM, with higher bids during peak hours).

## 4. Customer Acquisition Channels — Referrals

### 4.1 Structured Referral Program — "Kannukkul Kirukku" (Joy in Eyes)

Word-of-mouth is the strongest customer acquisition channel in small-town India, where trust is built through personal relationships and community reputation. We will formalise this organic referral behaviour into a structured "Kannukkul Kirukku" Referral Program that rewards existing customers for bringing new customers. The program mechanics are simple and tangible:

- **Referrer Reward:** When an existing customer refers a new customer who makes a purchase, the referrer receives a Rs. 200 discount voucher on their next purchase OR a free premium lens cleaning kit worth Rs. 300.
- **New Customer Welcome:** The referred new customer receives a 10% discount on their first purchase AND a free eye examination (normally priced at Rs. 200).
- **Referral Cards:** We will print 1,000 referral cards (similar to business card size) with the referrer's name handwritten on the back. Each card has a unique code linked to the referrer in our system.
- **Tracking:** Our billing software will track referral codes to attribute new customers to their referrer. Monthly reports will identify our top referrers.
- **VIP Recognition:** Customers who refer 5 or more new customers in a year will be designated as "Vision Ambassadors" and receive additional perks including priority service, exclusive preview of new collections, and an annual gift.

**Why This Works in Sankarankovil:** In a close-knit community like Sankarankovil, where families, friends, and neighbours frequently share recommendations for service providers, a referral program that provides tangible rewards simply formalises existing behaviour. The key is making the reward feel generous enough to motivate action while keeping the CAC affordable. At Rs. 200 per referral reward plus the 10% discount for the new customer (average discount of Rs. 150), the total cost per referred customer is approximately Rs. 350, which is well within acceptable CAC for a customer whose lifetime value is Rs. 2,500 to Rs. 5,000.

### 4.2 Digital Referral — WhatsApp Share & Earn

We will create a simple WhatsApp-forwardable digital referral card that existing customers can share with their contacts. The card will feature a festive design, our branding, a QR code linking to WhatsApp, and an offer message: "My trusted optical shop — Sankaran Kovil Opticals. Show this message for a free eye test and 10% off on your first purchase!" Every new customer who arrives via this WhatsApp share will be tracked, and the referring customer will automatically receive their reward via WhatsApp message. This digitises the referral process, making it effortless for customers to refer while providing us with trackable attribution.

### 4.3 Professional Referral Network — Doctor & Teacher Partnerships

We will build a referral network with local general physicians, paediatricians, and school teachers who are often the first point of contact when families notice vision problems. Each referring professional will receive a framed "Referral Partner" certificate for their clinic, a quarterly supply of eye health pamphlets co-branded with our shop to distribute to their patients, and a Rs. 100 gift for every patient they refer who makes a purchase. This professional referral network is particularly powerful in a small town where doctors are highly trusted community figures.

## 5. Customer Acquisition Channels — Partnerships

### 5.1 School Partnerships

Schools are the single most strategic partnership channel for an optical shop. Research from Tamil Nadu shows that approximately 20.4% of school children have refractive errors requiring correction, yet many go undiagnosed due to lack of screening. Every child identified with a vision problem is a potential customer, but more importantly, they bring their parents, siblings, and sometimes grandparents into our shop. We will partner with 10 to 15 schools within a 15-km radius of Sankarankovil (including Government schools, private schools, and the Government Arts College) to conduct annual vision screening programs.

**Partnership Terms:** We offer free vision screening for all students using a portable visual acuity chart and auto-refractor. Schools receive a framed "Eye Health Partner" certificate, free eye health awareness sessions for students, and first-aid eye care kits for the school infirmary. In return, we receive permission to distribute our branded materials to parents during parent-teacher meetings, and parents of children identified with vision problems receive a referral card directing them to our shop for a comprehensive eye examination. Each school partnership generates an estimated 20 to 50 new customer visits per screening cycle.

### 5.2 Corporate & Institutional Partnerships

Sankarankovil and surrounding areas have several institutions that employ 50 to 500 people — government offices (Taluk office, Revenue department, BSNL), banks (SBI, Canara Bank, Indian Bank, cooperative banks), agricultural cooperatives, spinning mills, and the Sankarankovil municipality. We will approach these institutions with a corporate eye health package that includes:

- **Free on-site eye screening camps** conducted at the institution's premises during working hours, minimising employee downtime
- **Special corporate discount cards** (10% to 15% off on all products) for all employees
- **Annual Vision Health Reports** summarising the eye health status of the workforce, which HR departments can use for their wellness program documentation
- **Family extension:** The corporate discount extends to employees' immediate family members, driving additional foot traffic

We will designate one staff member as our Corporate Liaison Officer responsible for building relationships with HR managers and institution heads. Each corporate partnership can generate 30 to 100 new customers in the first year.

### 5.3 Healthcare Provider Partnerships

We will establish formal partnerships with ophthalmologists and eye hospitals in Tirunelveli and Tenkasi (the nearest cities with specialist eye care facilities). Under this arrangement, we serve as the optical dispensing partner for these specialists' patients who need spectacles after consultation. The referring doctor receives a nominal referral fee (as per industry norms), and patients receive seamless service — they get their medical consultation at the hospital and their spectacles at our convenient Sankarankovil location. This B2B2C partnership model positions us as the preferred dispensing optician for the region.

### 5.4 Local Business Cross-Promotion Partnerships

We will create a "Sankarankovil Wellness Circle" — a coalition of 5 to 8 complementary local businesses (general physician clinic, dental clinic, pharmacy, gym/fitness centre, health food store, salon) that cross-promote each other. Each business displays the others' promotional materials and offers a joint loyalty card — customers who visit any 3 of the partner businesses within 3 months receive a combined discount of 15%. This cross-pollination strategy expands each business's customer reach at virtually zero incremental cost.

## 6. New Customer Onboarding Process

### 6.1 Pre-Visit Onboarding (Digital Touchpoints)

The new customer journey begins before they enter our shop. Every touchpoint — from the Google listing they see online to the WhatsApp message they receive after enquiring — must be carefully designed to build confidence and reduce purchase anxiety. Here is our step-by-step pre-visit onboarding process:

**Step 1 — Discovery & First Impression:** A potential customer discovers us through any of our acquisition channels (pamphlet, referral, Google search, social media, etc.). At this stage, they may call us or send a WhatsApp message to enquire. Our response time target is under 15 minutes during business hours. The greeting message should be warm, in the customer's preferred language (Tamil or English), and informative: "Vanakkam! Welcome to Sankaran Kovil Opticals. We offer comprehensive eye testing, branded frames, lenses, and sunglasses. Our optometrist is available from 9 AM to 8 PM. Would you like to book an appointment?"

**Step 2 — Appointment Booking (Optional):** For customers who prefer scheduled visits, we offer appointment booking via phone, WhatsApp, or Google. Appointments reduce perceived waiting time and make the visit feel premium. We will reserve 2 dedicated appointment slots per hour (with the remaining slots available for walk-ins). Appointed customers receive a WhatsApp confirmation with the date, time, a brief description of what to expect, and a map link.

**Step 3 — Pre-Visit WhatsApp Message:** 24 hours before the appointment (or at the time of enquiry for walk-in customers who have shared their number), we send a pre-visit message: "We look forward to seeing you tomorrow! Our address is [address]. Tip: If you currently wear glasses, please bring them along. If you have a previous prescription, that would help our optometrist too. See you soon!" This message serves as a reminder, sets expectations, and shows organisational professionalism.

### 6.2 In-Store Onboarding

**Step 1 — Warm Welcome:** When a new customer enters the shop, they are greeted within 10 seconds by a staff member with a smile and a "Vanakkam, welcome to Sankaran Kovil Opticals." The customer is offered a glass of water (a simple but deeply appreciated gesture in Tamil Nadu culture) and seated comfortably in the waiting area if the optometrist is occupied.

**Step 2 — Registration:** A new customer registration form captures: full name, phone number, WhatsApp number, age, occupation, area of residence, how they heard about us (referral source tracking), and whether they have any existing eye conditions. This data is entered into our customer database immediately. Registration takes under 3 minutes and is framed as a one-time process that enables us to provide personalised service in future visits.

**Step 3 — Customer Identification Card:** Every new customer receives a laminated "Sankaran Kovil Opticals Vision Card" — a wallet-sized card with their name, customer ID number, date of registration, and our contact information. The card has a magnetic strip or barcode that can be scanned for quick identification during future visits. This card creates a sense of belonging and makes future visits frictionless.

**Step 4 — Eye Examination:** The optometrist introduces themselves, explains the process, and conducts a thorough eye examination. After the examination, the optometrist explains the results in simple Tamil, answering all questions patiently. If a prescription is needed, it is explained in detail, including lens type recommendations.

**Step 5 — Frame Selection & Purchase:** A trained sales associate guides the customer through frame selection, considering face shape, lifestyle, budget, and prescription requirements. There is zero pressure to purchase — the customer is encouraged to try multiple frames. After selection, lens options and pricing are explained transparently.

### 6.3 Post-Visit Onboarding

**Within 2 Hours:** A thank-you WhatsApp message is sent: "Thank you for visiting Sankaran Kovil Opticals! We hope you had a great experience. Your glasses will be ready on [date]. We will WhatsApp you when they're ready. In the meantime, please don't hesitate to reach out if you have any questions."

**On Delivery Day:** A WhatsApp message notifies the customer that their glasses are ready, with a reminder of the shop hours. When they collect their glasses, the optometrist ensures proper fitting and adjustment, and explains lens care instructions.

**7 Days Post-Purchase:** A follow-up WhatsApp message checks on comfort and satisfaction: "It's been a week since you got your new glasses. How are they feeling? If you need any adjustment, please visit us anytime — adjustments are free for life!"

**30 Days Post-Purchase:** A satisfaction survey (3 questions via WhatsApp) and an invitation to leave a Google review: "We'd love to hear about your experience! Please share your feedback and help others find us on Google."

## 7. First Visit Experience Design

### 7.1 The "Wow" Moment — Entrance & Ambience

The first visit is the most critical moment in the customer acquisition journey. A customer who walks into a clean, well-organised, and welcoming shop is 70% more likely to make a purchase and return. Our shop entrance must be inviting and clearly branded. The exterior will feature a prominent illuminated signboard with the shop name in both Tamil and English, a clean glass door with the shop timings, and a small A-board on the pavement with an attractive offer ("Free Eye Test Today").

Inside, the shop is air-conditioned (a significant differentiator in Sankarankovil where few retail shops offer air conditioning), well-lit with warm white LED lighting that makes frames look attractive without causing glare, and pleasantly scented with a mild room freshener. The walls feature elegant frame displays organised by brand and price range. A dedicated children's corner with a small stool and colouring books keeps young children engaged while parents browse. Background music (soft Tamil film instrumentals or light classical music) creates a relaxed atmosphere. A small display screen near the entrance shows a loop of eye health tips and customer testimonials in Tamil.

### 7.2 The Consultation Experience

The eye examination room is the heart of our shop and must feel clinical yet comfortable. It is equipped with modern equipment (auto-refractor, trial lens set, Snellen chart, slit lamp if available) that is clean and well-maintained. The optometrist wears a clean professional coat and name badge. The consultation is conducted with the curtain drawn for privacy. The optometrist speaks in the customer's preferred language, uses analogies to explain technical concepts (e.g., "Imagine your eye is like a camera; the lens helps focus light on the film, which is your retina"), and provides a printed prescription card in a branded folder.

**Time Investment:** First-visit consultations are allocated 20 to 30 minutes — significantly longer than the 5-minute exams at competitor shops. This time investment signals thoroughness and builds deep trust, which is essential in a market where customers are sceptical of quick-check optical shops. The optometrist also asks about screen time, lifestyle, and family eye health history, demonstrating genuine care for the patient's wellbeing.

### 7.3 Frame Selection — Guided, Not Pressured

Frame selection can be overwhelming for first-time buyers or customers unfamiliar with current trends. Our sales associates are trained in face shape analysis and style consultation. They guide the customer through a curated selection rather than showing the entire inventory. For male customers, the associate suggests 5 to 8 frames based on face shape and occupation. For female customers, the range includes both everyday-wear and occasion-wear options. For children, the focus is on durability and comfort. At every stage, the associate provides honest feedback: "This one suits your face shape very well" or "This frame is stylish but may be too wide for your prescription — let me suggest a smaller option." This honest, consultative approach builds immense trust and differentiates us from commission-driven chain stores.

### 7.4 Transparent Pricing & Payment Options

Pricing is a major anxiety point for optical customers in India, where the industry is perceived as opaque. We address this through: (a) Frame price tags displayed clearly on every frame, (b) A printed lens menu listing all lens types with their features and prices, (c) A "Total Price Calculator" on a tablet that shows the combined frame + lens cost before the customer commits, (d) EMI options for purchases above Rs. 3,000 (partnering with a local finance provider), and (e) Acceptance of UPI (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm), cash, debit/credit cards, and even older customers' preferred payment methods.

### 7.5 The Departure Experience

When the customer leaves (whether or not they purchased), they are thanked genuinely, offered a visiting card, and told: "Even if you don't need glasses today, please come back anytime for a free eye test. We are here for your eye health." Non-buyers receive a follow-up WhatsApp after 3 days with a soft reminder: "We hope you're doing well! If you'd like to revisit your options or have any questions about your eye test results, we're always here." This no-pressure follow-up often converts hesitant first-time visitors into customers within a week.

## 8. Lead Generation Tactics

### 8.1 Free Eye Checkup as Lead Magnet

The single most effective lead magnet for an optical shop is a free comprehensive eye examination. In Sankarankovil, many residents have never had a proper eye test, relying instead on self-diagnosis or buying ready-made reading glasses from street vendors. Our "Free Eye Test" offer lowers the barrier to entry dramatically. Every free eye test generates a detailed patient record in our database, identifies customers who need prescription glasses (conversion opportunity), and captures contact information for future remarketing. We estimate that 40% to 60% of free eye test recipients will purchase at least one product (glasses, sunglasses, contact lenses) within 3 months.

**Implementation:** The free eye test offer will be promoted through every channel — pamphlets, banners, wall paintings, WhatsApp, Google, Facebook, auto ads, and school/corporate camps. We will designate specific time slots (2 PM to 4 PM, Monday to Thursday) as "Free Eye Test Hours" to manage capacity without disrupting paid consultation flow.

### 8.2 "Eye Health Score" — Gamified Lead Capture

We will create a simple "Eye Health Score" quiz — a 10-question self-assessment available on WhatsApp (via a chatbot or direct messaging) that evaluates the respondent's eye health based on symptoms like headaches, blurred vision, eye strain, screen time, age, and family history. The quiz takes 2 minutes to complete and generates a personalised "Eye Health Score" out of 100 along with recommendations. Respondents who score below 70 are advised to visit us for a detailed examination and offered a discount code. This gamified approach is engaging, shareable, and generates qualified leads who are already aware they may have a vision problem.

### 8.3 Seasonal Lead Campaigns

We will design lead generation campaigns tied to seasonal events and cultural moments:

- **Back to School (April-May):** "Is your child seeing clearly? Free vision screening for all school students. Book now!" Targeting parents of school-aged children.
- **Aadi Thapasu Festival (July-August):** "Temple Visit Special — Free eye test for all devotees. Get blessed with clear vision!" Tied to the Sankaranarayana Swamy Temple festival.
- **World Sight Day (Second Thursday of October):** "On World Sight Day, let's promise to protect our vision. Free comprehensive eye test all week." Leveraging a global awareness day for local promotion.
- **Diwali (October-November):** "Gift your family the gift of clear vision this Diwali. Special family packages available." Positioning eyeglasses as a thoughtful Diwali gift.
- **Senior Citizens' Day (October 1):** "Free eye checkup for all senior citizens above 60. Special discounts on reading glasses." Targeting the elderly population.
- **Pongal (January):** "New Year, New Vision. Start 2026 with a comprehensive eye test." Leveraging the Tamil New Year sentiment.

Each seasonal campaign includes dedicated pamphlets, social media creatives, WhatsApp broadcasts, and a specific landing page or GMB post.

### 8.4 Data Capture at Every Touchpoint

Every customer interaction — whether in-person, phone, or WhatsApp — should result in data capture. We will implement a strict policy: "No interaction without data capture." This means: walk-in customers are always registered (name + phone minimum), phone enquiries are logged, WhatsApp conversations are saved with labels, and event participants fill a brief registration form. Over 12 months, this data accumulation creates a powerful marketing database of 3,000 to 5,000 contacts that can be segmented and remarketed.

### 8.5 Community Notice Board & QR Codes

We will place branded community notice boards at 5 high-traffic locations — the temple notice area, bus stand, main market, railway station waiting room, and the Taluk Office complex. These notice boards feature eye health tips, our shop information, a QR code linking to our Google Maps location and WhatsApp, and a tear-off strip with our phone number. This passive lead generation mechanism works 24/7, capturing interest from people who may not actively be looking for an optical shop but are prompted by the educational content.

## 9. School Vision Screening Programs

### 9.1 Program Overview & Rationale

School vision screening is arguably the most impactful customer acquisition initiative for an optical shop in a semi-urban area like Sankarankovil. Research published in the Indian Journal of Community Medicine shows that the prevalence of refractive errors among school children in Tamil Nadu is approximately 20.4%, with myopia (nearsightedness) being the most common type at 10.8%. These children need corrective eyeglasses, and their parents are willing buyers when made aware of the problem by a credible source. More importantly, each identified child brings at least one parent to our shop — and parents themselves may discover they need glasses during the visit.

The Government of India launched the National Program for Control of Blindness & Visual Impairment (NPCBVI) in 1976, which includes school eye screening. However, government screening programs in Tier-3 and Tier-4 towns are often under-resourced and irregular. By filling this gap with a professionally executed, regular screening program, Sankaran Kovil Opticals positions itself as a community health partner while generating a steady stream of new customers.

### 9.2 Program Design

**Target Schools:** We will approach 10 to 15 schools within a 15-km radius, including:
- Government Higher Secondary School, Sankarankovil
- Government Girls Higher Secondary School, Sankarankovil
- CSI Primary School, Sankarankovil
- Hindu Nadar Primary School, Sankarankovil
- Montfort School (if applicable)
- Schools in Puliangudi, Kadayanallur, and surrounding villages

**Screening Methodology:** Each screening session covers one school and is conducted during school hours with prior permission from the school administration. We use a portable Snellen chart (tumbling E chart for younger children, standard letter chart for older children), a handheld auto-refractor for quick assessment, and a torch for external eye examination. Each student is screened in under 3 minutes. Students who fail the screening (visual acuity worse than 6/9 in either eye) receive a "Vision Report Card" — a colourful, child-friendly document explaining the screening result, addressed to their parents, with a recommendation to visit Sankaran Kovil Opticals for a detailed examination.

**Staffing:** Each screening session requires 2 team members — our optometrist and one sales associate. A screening session covering 200 students takes approximately 4 hours. We will conduct 2 screening sessions per month during the academic year (June to March), covering all partner schools in a 10-month cycle.

### 9.3 Conversion Path

The conversion funnel for school screening is: Screened student → Parent receives Vision Report Card → Parent brings child to shop for detailed examination → Child needs glasses → Purchase. Our historical benchmark suggests that 60% to 70% of parents of children identified with vision problems will bring their child for a follow-up visit within 30 days, and 80% of those will purchase glasses. From a school of 500 students, we can expect to identify approximately 100 children with vision issues, leading to 60 to 70 shop visits and 48 to 56 eyeglass purchases — plus additional purchases from accompanying parents and siblings.

### 9.4 School-Specific Marketing Materials

For each school screening, we create branded materials including: a banner announcing the screening date (to be displayed at the school gate), a certificate of participation for the school, individual Vision Report Cards for each student screened, parent information handouts about children's eye health, and branded pencils or erasers distributed to all students (inexpensive but effective brand reminders). Total cost per school screening session is approximately Rs. 2,000 to Rs. 3,000 (excluding staff time), making this one of the lowest-CAC acquisition channels available.

### 9.5 Long-Term Impact

School screening creates a multi-generational customer pipeline. A child who gets their first pair of glasses from us at age 8 becomes a customer for life — requiring new glasses every 1 to 2 years as their prescription changes, transitioning to contact lenses in their teens, and becoming a fashion-conscious sunglasses buyer in their twenties. Each school screening plants the seeds for 10 to 15 years of recurring revenue.

Each employee is screened in 5 to 8 minutes, receiving an instant vision report card. Employees who need detailed examination receive a referral card for our shop with a special corporate discount (15% off). We also provide a summary report to the HR department listing the number of employees screened, common issues identified, and recommendations for workplace eye health.

Within 48 hours, send a WhatsApp broadcast to all screened employees reminding them of their referral offer. Within 1 week, follow up individually with employees who were identified as needing glasses. Within 1 month, send a satisfaction survey to the institution. Track conversion rates and attribute revenue to each corporate camp.

This translates to Rs. 20,000 to Rs. 70,000 in revenue per camp. With 2 camps per month, corporate programs can contribute Rs. 40,000 to Rs. 1,40,000 per month in revenue while acquiring 20 to 40 new customers monthly.

## 11. Temple & Festival Event Marketing

### 11.1 The Sankarankovil Temple Advantage

The Arulmigu Sankaranarayana Swamy Temple in Sankarankovil is one of the most significant temples in southern Tamil Nadu. This unique temple is dedicated to the combined form of Shiva and Vishnu (Sankaranarayanan), and is closely associated with the Gomathi Amman shrine. The temple's annual festivals draw tens of thousands of devotees from across Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and other parts of India. Key festival events include:

- **Aadi Thapasu (July/Aadi Month):** A 10-day festival commemorating the penance of Gomathi Amman. This is the biggest annual event, attracting an estimated 50,000 to 1,00,000 pilgrims over 10 days. The festival includes the famous "Ther Thiruvizha" (chariot festival) and "Theppam" (float festival) in the temple tank.
- **Panguni Uthiram (March):** A major festival celebrating the divine wedding, attracting 30,000 to 50,000 devotees over 3 to 5 days.
- **Monthly Krithigai & Amavasya:** Regular monthly special pooja days that bring 5,000 to 10,000 devotees each.
- **Karthigai Deepam (November/December):** The festival of lights, bringing 20,000 to 30,000 visitors.
- **Thai Poosam (January/February):** Another significant festival drawing 15,000 to 25,000 devotees.

This temple footfall represents a massive, concentrated customer acquisition opportunity. Unlike regular marketing that reaches dispersed audiences, temple festival marketing allows us to access thousands of potential customers in a defined geographic area over a short period.

### 11.2 Temple Area Marketing Strategy

**Permanent Presence:** We will establish a permanent branded kiosk or banner within 100 metres of the temple entrance (subject to local municipality regulations and temple authority permissions). This kiosk will feature our branding, service highlights, and a QR code for WhatsApp contact. It serves as a 24/7 passive marketing asset.

**Festival-Specific Booth:** During major festivals (Aadi Thapasu, Panguni Uthiram, Karthigai Deepam), we will set up a temporary promotional booth or canopy near the temple entrance. This booth will offer free vision screening using a portable chart (2-minute screening), distribute eye health pamphlets with a festival-themed cover, and issue discount coupons valid for 30 days. The booth will be staffed by 2 team members from 7 AM to 9 PM during festival days. We estimate 200 to 500 people will visit our booth per festival day, generating 30 to 100 leads per day.

**Temple-Adjacent Wall Painting:** A large, professionally painted wall advertisement on a building wall facing the temple approach road, visible to every pilgrim entering or exiting the temple complex. This is a high-impact, long-term investment.

### 11.3 Temple Prasad & Offering Integration

With the temple administration's permission, we can explore distributing small packets of eye-friendly prasad (e.g., dry fruits like almonds and raisins, which are beneficial for eye health) branded with our shop name and a message like "Sankaran Kovil Opticals wishes you divine vision — both spiritual and physical." This is a culturally sensitive approach that adds value to pilgrims while creating positive brand association. We can also sponsor annadhanam (free food distribution) on one day during Aadi Thapasu, with our branding on the food distribution counter.

### 11.4 Pilgrim Route Marketing

Many pilgrims travel to Sankarankovil via specific routes — from Tenkasi (14 km), Tirunelveli (56 km), Madurai (130 km), and Rajapalayam (60 km). We will place wall paintings and banners along these approach roads, particularly on the Tenkasi-Sankarankovil road (the primary approach). Pilgrims from Kerala travelling via the Shenkottai route will see our advertisements on the Shenkottai-Sankarankovil road. These route advertisements target the floating population of pilgrims who may not have a local optical shop and are receptive to discovering a reliable option during their visit.

### 11.5 Festival WhatsApp Campaign

During each major festival, we will run a dedicated WhatsApp campaign targeting our existing contact database and community groups: "Sankarankovil temple festival special! Visit Sankaran Kovil Opticals during the festival week and receive a free eye test + 15% off on all frames. Nandri! 🙏" We will also encourage our existing customers to share this message with their temple-visit groups and family WhatsApp groups, creating organic viral reach.

## 15. Acquisition Funnel Design

### 15.1 Funnel Stages

The customer acquisition funnel for Sankaran Kovil Opticals consists of five distinct stages, each requiring specific strategies, metrics, and optimisation efforts:

```
AWARENESS → INTEREST → CONSIDERATION → CONVERSION → RETENTION
  (100%)     (60%)       (30%)          (15%)         (80%)
```

### 15.2 Stage 1: Awareness (Top of Funnel)

**Objective:** Make 100% of the target population in Sankarankovil and surrounding 20-km radius aware of Sankaran Kovil Opticals.

**Target Audience:** Approximately 2,00,000 people within the service area (Sankarankovil town population 70,000 + surrounding villages and towns within 20 km).

**Channels:** Wall paintings (permanent visibility), banners & hoardings, auto-rickshaw advertisements, temple area marketing, cable TV scrolling ads, newspaper advertisements, social media presence, Google Maps listing, event sponsorships.

**Metrics:** Impressions (estimated from channel reach), brand recall surveys (quarterly phone surveys of 100 random local residents asking "Can you name an optical shop in Sankarankovil?"), social media follower count, Google Maps views.

**Target:** 80% unaided brand awareness within 12 months (i.e., 80 out of 100 randomly surveyed residents can name Sankaran Kovil Opticals without prompting).

**Strategies:** The key at this stage is frequency and ubiquity. A person should encounter our brand at least 7 times across different touchpoints before they develop enough familiarity to consider us. This means our wall paintings, auto ads, banners, social media posts, and WhatsApp messages all work together to create this "surround sound" effect. In a small town, this is achievable within 3 to 6 months of consistent marketing.

### 15.3 Stage 2: Interest (Middle of Funnel)

**Objective:** Convert 60% of aware audience into people who have expressed interest (visited our shop, called, sent a WhatsApp message, visited our Google listing, or engaged with our social media).

**Channels:** Free eye test offer, educational content (eye health tips, videos), seasonal campaigns, school screening referral cards, corporate camp referral cards, social media engagement (comments, shares, saves), Google listing visits and direction requests.

**Metrics:** Shop walk-ins (tracked via door counter), phone calls received, WhatsApp messages received, Google listing actions (calls, direction requests, website clicks), social media engagement rate, pamphlet coupon redemptions.

**Target:** 300 to 500 interested prospects per month by Month 6.

**Strategies:** The free eye test is our primary interest generator — it removes the financial barrier and signals that we prioritise eye health over sales. Educational content on social media and WhatsApp positions us as experts, creating trust that converts awareness into curiosity. School and corporate screening programs generate pre-qualified interest from people who already know they may need glasses.

### 15.4 Stage 3: Consideration (Lower Middle of Funnel)

**Objective:** Convert 50% of interested prospects into active consideration (i.e., they visit our shop for an eye test or frame browsing).

**Channels:** First-visit experience design, WhatsApp reminders, Google reviews and ratings, referral recommendations from friends/family, compelling in-store promotions, proximity convenience (we are closer than competitors in Tenkasi or Tirunelveli).

**Metrics:** First-time visitor count (tracked via registration system), first-visit-to-consultation conversion rate, average time spent in shop, number of frames tried, brochure/catalogue requests.

**Target:** 150 to 250 first-time shop visitors per month by Month 6.

**Strategies:** The first-visit experience (detailed in Section 7) is the primary consideration driver. When a prospect enters a clean, air-conditioned, well-organised shop with a friendly, professional team, their consideration immediately deepens. Transparent pricing, honest frame recommendations, and a thorough eye examination differentiate us from competitors and tip the balance toward purchase.

### 15.5 Stage 4: Conversion (Bottom of Funnel)

**Objective:** Convert 50% of first-time visitors into paying customers (i.e., they purchase at least one product during their first visit or within 30 days).

**Channels:** In-store sales process, frame selection consultation, pricing and payment options, limited-time offers, post-visit follow-up messages for non-buyers.

**Metrics:** First-visit conversion rate, average transaction value (ATV), product mix (frames vs. sunglasses vs. contact lenses), payment method mix, return rate.

**Target:** 50% first-visit conversion rate, Rs. 2,500 average transaction value, generating 75 to 125 new purchasing customers per month by Month 6.

**Strategies:** The conversion strategy focuses on removing purchase friction: (a) Clear, transparent pricing eliminates anxiety, (b) A wide range across price points (Rs. 499 to Rs. 5,000+ frames) ensures affordability for all budgets, (c) EMI options reduce sticker shock for premium products, (d) A "try at home" option for customers who want to consult family before deciding, (e) Post-visit WhatsApp follow-up for non-buyers with a soft nudge and a time-limited extension of their first-visit offer.

### 15.6 Stage 5: Retention (Post-Funnel)

**Objective:** Retain 80% of first-year customers for repeat purchases and convert them into referrers.

**Channels:** Post-purchase follow-up (7-day and 30-day WhatsApp check-ins), annual reminder for eye examination, loyalty program, referral program, birthday and anniversary messages, exclusive previews of new collections, free lifetime adjustments.

**Metrics:** Customer retention rate (percentage of Year 1 customers who make a purchase in Year 2), referral rate (percentage of customers who refer at least one new customer), Net Promoter Score (NPS), repeat purchase frequency.

**Target:** 80% retention rate, 25% referral rate, NPS of 50+.

**Strategies:** Retention begins with the post-purchase experience — thorough fitting, care instructions, and follow-up contact. It is sustained through the "Kannukkul Kirukku" referral program, WhatsApp engagement (regular tips and offers), and a simple loyalty structure (every 5th purchase gets 20% off). In a small town, retention is easier than in cities because customers have fewer alternatives and brand loyalty is stronger. The key is to make every customer feel valued individually.

### 15.7 Funnel Optimisation — Monthly Review Process

We will conduct a monthly funnel review meeting involving the owner, optometrist, and sales associates to analyse:
- How many people entered the shop? (Door counter data)
- How many were first-time visitors? (Registration data)
- How many first-time visitors converted to customers? (Billing data)
- What was the average transaction value? (Billing data)
- How many customers were acquired from each channel? (Referral code tracking)
- What was the CAC for each channel? (Cost / new customer ratio)
- What is the funnel drop-off rate at each stage? (Stage metrics comparison)
- Which channels need more investment? Which are underperforming?

This data-driven approach ensures continuous optimisation of the acquisition funnel and prevents wasteful spending on underperforming channels.
