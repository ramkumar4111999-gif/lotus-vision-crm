## 6. Break-Even Analysis

The break-even point is the revenue level at which total revenue equals total costs (fixed + variable), resulting in zero profit or loss.

### 6.1 Break-Even Formula

```
Break-Even Revenue = Fixed Costs / (1 - Variable Cost Ratio)
```

- Monthly Fixed Costs = ₹87,000
- Variable Cost Ratio (COGS + commissions + payment charges) ≈ 45% of revenue = 0.45
- Contribution Margin = 1 - 0.45 = 0.55

```
Break-Even Revenue = 87,000 / 0.55 = ₹1,58,182/month
```

### 6.2 Break-Even Interpretation

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Monthly Break-Even Revenue | ₹1,58,182 |
| Daily Break-Even Revenue | ₹5,273 |
| Break-Even (Eyeglasses Pairs) | ~70 pairs/month |
| Break-Even (Total Transactions) | ~85 customers/month |
| **Expected Break-Even Month** | **Month 7 (October 2025)** |

### 6.3 Break-Even Timeline (Moderate Scenario)

The shop does not immediately break even because:

1. **Months 1–3:** Revenue is below ₹1,00,000. The shop is building awareness. Cash losses accumulate.
2. **Months 4–6:** Revenue climbs to ₹1,00,000–₹1,30,000 as word-of-mouth spreads and referral cycles activate. Losses narrow.

## 10. Key Assumptions & Risk Factors

### 10.1 Key Assumptions

1. **Location:** Shop is on the main road with adequate foot traffic. If the location is sub-optimal, revenue could be 20–30% lower.
2. **Competition:** Sankarankovil has 1–2 existing optical shops. If a major chain (Titan Eye+, Lenskart) enters the market, it could pressure margins.
3. **Optometrist Availability:** A qualified optometrist is available at ₹25,000/month. In smaller towns, this may require recruiting from nearby cities and providing accommodation.
4. **Supplier Credit:** Essilor and Zeiss distributors offer 15–30 day credit terms after 3–6 months of consistent ordering.
5. **Seasonality:** Revenue follows predictable seasonal patterns — school reopening (Jun–Aug), Diwali (Oct–Nov), and summer sunwear (Mar–May) are peak periods.
6. **Government Schemes:** Tamil Nadu's free spectacle scheme for school children and elderly may affect commercial demand, but also creates awareness and upgrade opportunities.
7. **Customer Behaviour:** Sankarankovil customers are price-sensitive but willing to pay for quality when trust is established. Word-of-mouth is the strongest acquisition channel.
8. **GST Compliance:** Revenue assumes GST-registered status with proper input tax credit claims on equipment and inventory purchases.

Sankaran Kovil Opticals presents a financially viable business opportunity with a clear path to profitability within 7–8 months of operation and full investment recovery within 18–24 months. The total startup investment of ₹12,00,000–₹13,50,000 (with enhanced working capital buffer) is modest relative to the 3-year cumulative profit potential of ₹18,84,000–₹30,60,000.

**Key Success Factors:**

1. **Location Quality** — A main-road shop near the temple, bus stand, or hospital area is non-negotiable for walk-in traffic.
2. **Optometrist Retention** — The optometrist is the single most important employee. Competitive compensation and a good working environment are essential.
3. **Trust Building** — In a small town, reputation is everything. Clinical accuracy, honest pricing, and excellent after-sales service will build a loyal customer base that generates referrals for years.
4. **Seasonal Planning** — The business is seasonal. Cash reserves built during peak months (Oct–Nov, Jun) must sustain operations during lean months (Aug, Feb).
5. **Inventory Discipline** — Dead stock is the silent killer of retail profitability. Implement strict inventory rotation and clearance protocols.
6. **Community Integration** — Sponsor local events, participate in health camps, and maintain visible community presence. This is more effective than paid advertising in small towns.

**Final Recommendation:** Proceed with the business, targeting a launch during **March–April 2025** to capture the school reopening season (June) and the Diwali festival (November) within the first year.
