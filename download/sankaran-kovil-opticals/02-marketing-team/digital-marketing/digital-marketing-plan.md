## 1. Digital Marketing Overview & Objectives

### 1.1 Current Situation Analysis

Sankaran Kovil Opticals operates in Sankarankovil, a tier-3 town in Tenkasi district, Tamil Nadu. The town has a population of approximately 70,000–80,000 people, with surrounding villages contributing to a serviceable market of roughly 2–3 lakh potential customers. The shop faces competition from 2–3 other local optical shops, travelling salespeople from chain brands like Lenskart and Titan Eye+, and increasingly from online eyewear platforms.

**Key Market Challenges:**
- Customers in small-town Tamil Nadu still rely heavily on word-of-mouth and physical store visits, but digital awareness is rapidly growing — especially among the 18–40 age group.
- Online players like Lenskart and Titan Eye+ are aggressively advertising in Tamil via Google and Meta ads, creating both competitive pressure and consumer education about the importance of regular eye check-ups and quality eyewear.
- Trust is the single biggest factor — customers need to feel confident about the accuracy of their eye test, the quality of lenses, and the durability of frames before making a purchase.
- Seasonal demand spikes occur around the academic season (June–August), festival periods (Diwali, Pongal), and wedding seasons — these need to be capitalized on with targeted campaigns.

**Digital Opportunity:**
According to 2025 industry data, India has over 780 million active internet users, with Tamil Nadu having one of the highest smartphone penetration rates in the country. Tier-2 and tier-3 towns are the fastest-growing segments for digital adoption. YouTube and WhatsApp are the dominant platforms in the Tamil-speaking demographic. Facebook remains strong among 30+ age groups, while Instagram is gaining rapid traction among the 18–30 youth segment. Google Search is the primary starting point for anyone looking for an "optical shop near me" or "eye hospital near me" in the Sankarankovil area.

### 1.4 Competitive Positioning

Our digital positioning will focus on three core pillars that online players cannot match:

1. **Trusted Expertise:** Highlight the optometrist's qualifications, years of experience, and advanced eye-testing equipment. Position the shop as a "trusted neighbourhood optical expert" — not just a retailer.
2. **Personalized Service:** Emphasize face-to-face consultations, frame-fitting sessions, after-sales service, and the ability to walk in anytime for adjustments — things online platforms fundamentally cannot provide.
3. **Local Convenience & Tamil Language:** All digital content will be in Tamil and English. Highlight easy accessibility, parking, and the comfort of dealing with a local business that understands the community.

## 2. Google My Business Optimization

### 2.1 Profile Setup & Verification

Google My Business (GMB) — now called Google Business Profile — is the single most important free digital marketing tool for any local business. For Sankaran Kovil Opticals, GMB optimization is critical because when someone in or around Sankarankovil searches "optical shop near me," "eye test near me," or "spectacle shop Sankarankovil," the Google Map Pack (top 3 results shown in the map) is the first thing they see. Ranking in this pack can drive 30–50 phone calls and direction requests per month — entirely free.

**Profile Completeness Checklist:**

- [ ] **Business Name:** Sankaran Kovil Opticals (exact, no keyword stuffing)
- [ ] **Primary Category:** Optical Shop / Eye Care Center
- [ ] **Secondary Categories:** Sunglasses Store, Contact Lens Supplier, Eyewear Supplier
- [ ] **Address:** Full address with pincode — 627756, Sankarankovil, Tenkasi District
- [ ] **Phone Number:** Primary business number (WhatsApp-enabled number preferred)
- [ ] **Website:** Link to the business website (to be built by web team)
- [ ] **Business Hours:** Monday–Saturday 9:00 AM – 8:00 PM, Sunday 10:00 AM – 2:00 PM
- [ ] **Attributes:** Women-led (if applicable), Free Wi-Fi, Wheelchair accessible, Accepts credit cards, Free parking
- [ ] **Products & Services List:** Complete listing of all frame brands, lens types (CR-39, Polycarbonate, Blue-cut, Progressive, Bifocal, Photochromic), sunglasses, contact lenses, eye testing services
- [ ] **Booking Link:** If applicable, integrate with an appointment booking system for eye tests

### 2.2 Photos & Visual Content Strategy

Google's algorithm heavily favours businesses that regularly upload fresh, high-quality photos. Studies show that businesses with 100+ photos receive 520% more calls than the average business, and listings with photos see 42% more direction requests.

**Photo Upload Plan (Weekly):**
- **Store Exterior:** Clear, well-lit photos of the shop front with the board visible — upload 5 new angles in Month 1, then refresh quarterly
- **Store Interior:** Wide shots showing the frame display, testing equipment, waiting area — upload 3–5 new photos monthly
- **Product Close-ups:** Individual frames, lens types, new collections — upload 10+ product photos weekly
- **Team Photos:** Photos of the optometrist and staff in professional attire — builds trust and humanizes the brand
- **Customer Photos (with permission):** Happy customers wearing their new glasses — excellent social proof
- **Behind-the-scenes:** Eye testing in progress, lens fitting, frame adjustments — educational content
- **Event & Festival Decorations:** Special decorations for Diwali, Pongal, etc. — shows the shop is active and community-oriented

**Target:** 200+ photos by end of Month 6, with 5–10 new photos added every single week.

### 2.3 Google Reviews Management

Online reviews are arguably the most powerful trust signal for local businesses. A 2025 survey shows that 87% of consumers read online reviews for local businesses, and 73% say that positive reviews make them trust a business more. For a small-town optical shop, reviews from fellow townspeople carry enormous weight.

**Review Generation Strategy:**

1. **In-Store Review Station:** Set up a simple QR code card at the billing counter that says "Scan to Review Us on Google." The QR code should directly open the GMB review page on the customer's phone. Train the billing staff to hand this to every customer after their purchase with a smile and say "சிறிது நேரம் ஒதுக்கி Google-ல் எங்களை review செய்யவும்" (Please take a moment to review us on Google).

2. **WhatsApp Follow-Up:** Within 24 hours of a customer's visit, send a WhatsApp message: "Thank you for visiting Sankaran Kovil Opticals! We'd love to hear about your experience. Please leave a review: [Google Review Link]. Your feedback helps us serve you better!"

3. **Offer Incentive (Ethical):** While Google's policies discourage incentivized reviews, you can offer a genuine post-purchase benefit: "Leave a Google review and get a free lens cleaning kit on your next visit!" This rewards the action without directly buying reviews.

4. **Respond to Every Review:** Respond to ALL reviews — positive and negative — within 24 hours. For positive reviews, thank them personally and mention the specific product or service. For negative reviews, apologize sincerely, offer to resolve the issue, and invite them back. Never argue with customers in public.

**Target:** Reach 50+ reviews by Month 3, 100+ by Month 6, and maintain a 4.5+ star average. If a negative review appears, address it immediately and work with the customer to resolve and potentially revise.

### 3.3 Google Local Services Ads

Google Local Services Ads (LSAs) — also known as Google Guaranteed — are specifically designed for local service businesses. They appear at the very top of Google search results, above regular ads and organic results, with a prominent "Google Guaranteed" badge. These ads are pay-per-lead, not pay-per-click, meaning you only pay when someone actually calls your business or books through the ad.

**LSA Setup for Sankaran Kovil Opticals:**
- Register as an "Eye Care Service" or "Optician" on Google Local Services
- Complete background check and verification (Google Guaranteed badge)
- Set maximum weekly budget (₹2,000–₹3,000 to start)
- Set maximum per-lead price (₹50–₹100 per qualified phone call)
- Target area: Sankarankovil + 25 km radius
- Key benefit: You only pay for actual phone calls, not clicks. This is extremely cost-effective for a local service business.

## 4. Facebook & Instagram Ad Campaigns

### 4.1 Platform Strategy & Rationale

Facebook and Instagram (Meta Ads) are the ideal platforms for Sankaran Kovil Opticals to build brand awareness, showcase products visually, and drive both online enquiries and in-store foot traffic. In Tamil Nadu's tier-3 towns, Facebook has massive penetration among the 25–55 age group — these are the decision-makers for family eyewear purchases. Instagram, meanwhile, is the fastest-growing platform among 18–30 year olds in the region, making it perfect for reaching students and young professionals who are highly fashion-conscious about their eyewear.

Unlike Google Ads (which captures existing demand), Meta Ads are demand-generation tools — they create awareness and desire among people who may not be actively searching for optical services but can be influenced to visit when they see an attractive offer or stylish frames. The visual nature of eyewear makes it a naturally strong fit for image and video-based platforms like Facebook and Instagram.

### 4.2 Ad Account Setup & Pixel Installation

**Critical First Steps:**
- Create a Meta Business Manager account
- Create a Facebook Business Page (if not already existing) — "Sankaran Kovil Opticals"
- Create an Instagram Business Account linked to the Facebook Page
- Install the Meta Pixel on the business website (coordinate with web team) — this tracks website visitors for retargeting
- Set up Conversion API (CAPI) alongside the Pixel for improved tracking accuracy
- Set up the WhatsApp Business API integration with Meta for lead generation via WhatsApp

**Audience Segmentation for Meta Ads:**

| Audience | Size (Est.) | Description |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Core Local | 50,000–80,000 | Sankarankovil + 20 km radius, Age 18–55 |
| Extended Local | 1,00,000–1,50,000 | Sankarankovil + 40 km radius (including Tenkasi, Tirunelveli outskirts) |
| Lookalike - Customers | 10,000–50,000 | Based on WhatsApp contact list of existing customers |
| Retargeting - Website Visitors | 1,000–5,000 | People who visited the website in the last 30 days |
| Retargeting - Engaged | 2,000–10,000 | People who engaged with FB/IG posts in the last 60 days |
| Interest-Based | 20,000–50,000 | People with interests in eyewear, fashion, health in the local area |

## 8. Retargeting Strategy

### 8.1 What is Retargeting & Why It Matters for Sankaran Kovil Opticals

Retargeting (also called remarketing) is the practice of showing ads to people who have already interacted with your business online — they visited your website, engaged with your social media, watched your YouTube video, or even visited your physical store (via location-based targeting). These "warm" audiences are far more likely to convert than cold audiences because they already know your brand.

Consider this scenario: A potential customer in Sankarankovil searches for "optical shop near me" on Google, sees your ad, clicks through to your website, browses your frame collection... but doesn't call or visit. Without retargeting, that customer is likely lost to a competitor or to online platforms. With retargeting, they will see your ads again on Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, and other websites — reminding them of your shop, building familiarity, and eventually driving them to visit.

Research shows that retargeted ads are 76% more likely to get clicks than regular display ads, and retargeting can increase conversion rates by up to 150%. For a local optical shop with a limited geographic market, every warm lead is incredibly valuable — losing them because of no retargeting is a significant waste of the initial ad spend that brought them to you.

### 10.6 ROI Calculation Framework

**Monthly ROI Formula:**
```
Marketing ROI (%) = (Revenue from Digital Customers - Digital Marketing Spend) / Digital Marketing Spend × 100
```

**Attribution Model:** Since Sankaran Kovil Opticals is primarily an offline business, we will use a "first-touch" attribution model with in-store tracking. Every customer who walks in should be asked: "How did you hear about us?" with options:
1. Google Search
2. Google Maps
3. Facebook
4. Instagram
5. WhatsApp
6. YouTube
7. Friend/Family referral
8. Walk-in (no digital touchpoint)
9. Other

Track this data in a simple spreadsheet and correlate with monthly marketing spend and campaign activity.

**Target ROI:** 200–400% by Month 6 (meaning every ₹1 spent on digital marketing should generate ₹2–₹4 in incremental revenue from customers who came through digital channels).

### 12.3 Key Success Factors

1. **Consistency:** The #1 reason small business digital marketing fails is inconsistency. Posting on social media for 2 weeks and then disappearing for a month is worse than not posting at all. Commit to the plan for a full 12 months before evaluating results.

2. **Tamil-First Content:** 80% of content should be in Tamil. This is not a suggestion — it is a requirement for success in the Sankarankovil market. English-only content will severely limit reach and engagement.

3. **Customer Experience Above All:** Digital marketing can bring people to the shop, but only a great in-store experience will bring them back. Ensure the shop is clean, well-organized, staff is friendly and knowledgeable, and the eye testing experience is professional and reassuring.

4. **Track Everything:** Every customer interaction that comes from a digital channel should be recorded. Without data, you cannot optimize. Without optimization, you cannot improve ROI.

5. **Patience:** Digital marketing is a compounding investment. Month 1–3 results may seem modest, but by Month 6–9, the accumulated effect of consistent effort across multiple platforms will create a powerful, self-reinforcing digital presence that drives a steady stream of new and returning customers.
