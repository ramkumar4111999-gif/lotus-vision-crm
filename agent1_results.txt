4. VADAKAASI AMMAN TEMPLE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  Note: "Vadakaasi Amman" appears to be a LOCAL STREET TEMPLE (Amman = 
  Mother Goddess shrine) that gives its name to the street (Vadakaasi Amman 
  Kovil Street / 2nd Street). The school is at address "67, Vadakaasi Amman 
  Kovil Street."

  This is NOT the famous Sankaranarayanaswamy/Gomathi Amman Temple (which is 
  the major temple in Sankarankovil). The Vadakaasi Amman is a smaller 
  neighborhood temple that generates LOCAL foot traffic only.

  HOWEVER, the FAMOUS Sankarankovil Temple (Gomathi Amman / Sankaranarayanaswamy):
  - 900 years old, built by Ukkira Pandian (Pandiya dynasty)
  - One of the most prominent temples in South Tamil Nadu
  - Major FESTIVAL: "Aadi Thapasu/Thabasu" - celebrated in Tamil month Aadi 
    (typically July-August)
  - Draws THOUSANDS of devotees from across Tamil Nadu during the festival
  - The Hindu newspaper reported "Thousands participate in Aadi Thavasu festival"
  - Temple located in town centre, walkable from bus stand
  - Annadhanam (free meals) provided daily to ~100 devotees
  
  FESTIVAL TRAFFIC IMPACT ON THIS LOCATION:
  - During Aadi Thapasu (July-August), the ENTIRE town sees massive pilgrim 
    footfall (thousands)
  - Processions pass through main roads including this area
  - Temporary spike in potential customers during festival period (5-10 days)
  - Many devotees from rural areas visit shops near temple routes

  FOOTFALL CONTRIBUTION: MODERATE (daily from local temple) + HIGH during festivals
