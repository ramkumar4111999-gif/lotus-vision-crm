SECTION 8: CUSTOMER BEHAVIOR PATTERNS
================================================================================

  PATTERN 1: VILLAGE-TO-TOWN SHOPPING CYCLE
  - 50+ surrounding villages depend on Sankarankovil for non-agricultural purchases
  - Villagers arrive by bus (103+ services), auto-rickshaw, or two-wheeler
  - Shopping route: Bus Stand → Main Road → Temple → Return
  - Main Road (Location 2) is ON this natural shopping route
  - Mela Bazar (Location 1) is NOT on the typical village shopper route

  PATTERN 2: TEMPLE + SHOPPING COMBO
  - Sankara Narayanaswamy Temple draws 2,000-4,000 daily visitors
  - Temple visitors combine pilgrimage with shopping (textiles, jewellery, food)
  - Main Road is the approach road — natural shopping happens here
  - Mela Bazar is NOT on the temple approach route

  PATTERN 3: DAILY COMMUTER SPENDING
  - 10,000-14,000 daily bus passengers pass through Main Road
  - Impulse purchases: Tea, snacks, mobile accessories, water, medicines
  - Main Road captures this transient spending; Mela Bazar does not

  PATTERN 4: STUDENT-DRIVEN SPENDING
  - 45+ schools = significant student population
  - School-related spending: Uniforms, bags, stationery, spectacles, sports goods
  - Students travel via Main Road; school zones near Main Road
  - No school near Mela Bazar area

  PATTERN 5: FESTIVAL & SEASONAL SPENDING SURGE
  - Pongal (Jan): All retail peaks
  - Chittirai Brahmotsavam (Apr-May): 41-day temple festival
  - Aadi Thapasu (Jul-Aug): 12-day temple festival
  - Deepavali (Oct-Nov): Massive retail surge
  - Festival shoppers overwhelmingly gravitate to Main Road
