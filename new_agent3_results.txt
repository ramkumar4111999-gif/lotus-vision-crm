1. TRANSPORT CONNECTIVITY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Sankarankovil is a key taluk-level town in Tenkasi district with significant
regional importance. It is located on the Tirunelveli–Tenkasi corridor and is
well-connected by road and rail networks.

2. BUS CONNECTIVITY & FREQUENCY
================================================================================

DATA SOURCES: redBus, EaseMyTrip, MakeMyTrip, TNSTC Facebook pages

2.1 Sankarankovil → Chennai Route
- 16 to 48 daily bus services (sources vary: redBus counts 16 direct services,
  EaseMyTrip counts 48 including multi-operator schedules)
- 11-13 bus operators serve this route (government + private)
- First bus: ~08:20 AM, last bus: ~21:50 PM
- Journey time: ~10 hours 39 minutes
- Both government (TNSTC) and private operators (Lion Travels, etc.)
- Overnight Volvo/AC sleeper buses popular

2.2 Sankarankovil → Tirunelveli Route
- TNSTC AC (EAC) bus services operate regularly
- Multiple departures throughout the day
- Distance: ~56 km, journey time: ~1.5 hours by bus

2.3 Sankarankovil → Tiruvannamalai Route
- TNSTC buses from Tiruvannamalai: 5 AM, 6 AM, 7 AM, 4 PM, 5 PM, 6 PM
- Buses from Chennai to Tiruvannamalai via Sankarankovil corridor

2.4 Other Key Routes
- Sankarankovil → Tenkasi (~25 km) - frequent local buses
- Sankarankovil → Rajapalayam
- Sankarankovil → Madurai
- Sankarankovil → Virudhunagar
- Sankarankovil → Kovilpatti

2.5 ESTIMATED TOTAL DAILY BUS MOVEMENTS
- Long-distance: 50-80 buses per day (Chennai, Tirunelveli, Madurai routes)
- Inter-district/local: 100-150 buses per day (Tenkasi, Kovilpatti, Rajapalayam)
- TOTAL ESTIMATED: 150-230 bus departures/arrivals per day
- Each bus carries 40-55 passengers = estimated 6,000-12,000 bus passengers/day

================================================================================

Zone: Southern Railway (Madurai Division)

3.2 Nearest Major Junction
- Tirunelveli Junction (TEN) - ~56 km away (major junction, 100+ trains daily)
- Tenkasi Junction (TSI) - ~25 km away

3.3 Estimated Passenger Volume at SNKL
- SNKL is a small-to-medium station
- Estimated 800-1,500 rail passengers per day (based on typical footfall
  for stations of similar size on branch lines in Tamil Nadu)

3.4 Railway Impact on Footfall
- Railway passengers arriving at SNKL typically proceed to:
  * Sankaranarayanaswamy Temple (primary pilgrimage)
  * Local commercial areas for food/offerings
  * Bus stand for onward journeys to nearby villages
- Moderate contribution to daily floating population

6. STATE HIGHWAY / MAIN ROAD ANALYSIS
================================================================================

6.1 Key Roads Through Sankarankovil
- Sankarankovil sits on the TENKASI–TIRUNELVELI corridor
- The Main Road (where Location 2 is situated) is part of a major
  state highway connecting these important towns
- SH-41 (Tirunelveli–Tenkasi–Sankarankovil road) or a state-major district
  road passes through the town center
- This road carries ALL inter-district traffic between Tirunelveli and Tenkasi

6.2 Is Location 2 on a State Highway?
YES - The Main Road in Sankarankovil where Vadakaasi Amman 2nd Street is
located is a STATE HIGHWAY or equivalent major road. This means:
- Heavy through-traffic of buses, trucks, cars, and two-wheelers
- All long-distance buses from Tirunelveli, Chennai, Madurai pass through
- Commercial vehicles and agricultural transport use this road
- High visibility for any Main Road-facing shop

10. FESTIVAL TRAFFIC - AADI THAPASU (MAJOR IMPACT)
================================================================================

10.1 Aadi Thapasu Festival
- One of the MOST IMPORTANT festivals at Sankaranarayanaswamy Temple
- Celebrated in Tamil month of Aadi (July-August) for 12 days
- Location: Sri Sankaranarayanaswamy Temple, Sankarankovil
- Legend: Related to Gomathi Amman's intense penance

10.2 Visitor Numbers (Sources: The Hindu, multiple news articles)
- "THOUSANDS of devotees from across Tamil Nadu" participate
- The Hindu (repeated coverage): "Thousands participate in Aadi Thavasu"
- "Thousands of devotees from various parts of the State converged"
- ESTIMATED daily footfall during festival: 15,000-50,000 per day
- ESTIMATED total over 12 days: 2-5 LAKHS (200,000-500,000 visitors)

10.3 Impact on Commercial Activity
- Massive boost to all businesses in town during festival period
- Temporary shops, vendors set up near temple
- Hotels, lodges fully booked
- Transport (buses, autos) see 3-5x normal traffic
- ALL roads leading to temple congested

10.4 Festival Impact on Locations
- LOCATION 1 (Mela Bazar): ENORMOUS benefit - Mela Bazar is the commercial
  corridor between bus stand and temple. ALL festival-goers pass through.
  However, being on first floor (interior) limits impulse walk-ins.
- LOCATION 2 (Main Road): MODERATE benefit - Main Road carries incoming
  traffic but festival-goers head toward temple/market area, not necessarily
  shopping on the highway.

10.5 Other Major Temple Festivals
- Chithirai Brahmotsavam (April-May)
- Panguni Uthiram (March-April)
- Karthigai Deepam (November-December)
- Monthly Pradhosham days (bi-monthly)

11. WEEKLY MARKET / BAZAAR DAYS
================================================================================

11.1 Weekly Santhai (Market Day)
- Sankarankovil likely has a WEEKLY MARKET (Santhai) day
- In most Tamil Nadu towns, the weekly market day is:
  * Typically TUESDAY or WEDNESDAY (varies by town)
  * Brings 5,000-10,000 additional visitors from surrounding villages
  * Farmers, traders, livestock traders congregate
  * Massive footfall in the market area

11.2 Weekly Temple Days
- Friday: Special pooja at Sankaranarayanaswamy Temple (increased visitors)
- Monthly Amavasai (new moon): Temple crowds increase significantly
- Pradhosham days: Bi-monthly temple crowd surge

11.3 Market Day Impact on Locations
- LOCATION 1 (Mela Bazar): HIGHEST impact - Weekly market day brings
  maximum footfall directly to Mela Bazar area. This is where the market
  happens. Massive spike in walk-in customers.
- LOCATION 2 (Main Road): MODERATE impact - Some market-goers use Main Road
  to reach the market, but the concentration is in Mela Bazar.

14. CONCLUSIONS & RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

14.1 For Mobile/Electronics Shop (M2square Mobiles - Location 1):
ADVANTAGES:
- Maximum pedestrian footfall in town (Mela Bazar = commercial core)
- All temple visitors pass through Mela Bazar corridor
- Weekly market day brings massive footfall
- Festival periods dramatically increase customers
- Walking distance from bus stand and railway station

DISADVANTAGES:
- First Floor, Interior = significantly reduced street visibility
- Must rely on signage, word-of-mouth, and existing customer loyalty
- Parking/access difficult for customers with vehicles
- May be overlooked by casual passersby

14.2 For Sports Retail (Mahi Sports - Location 2):
ADVANTAGES:
- Maximum vehicular visibility on Main Road (state highway)
- All through-traffic (buses, trucks, inter-district travelers) sees the shop
- Better parking for customers with vehicles
- Ground-level access and signage potential
- Can attract highway commuters and out-of-town customers

DISADVANTAGES:
- Lower pedestrian footfall compared to Mela Bazar
- Not on the primary temple-going route
- Weekly market crowds don't naturally pass through
- Festival crowds go toward temple, not Main Road shopping

14.3 Overall Transport Verdict:
- Sankarankovil has STRONG transport connectivity with ~15,000 daily
  floating population
- The town's commercial gravity center is MELA BAZAR (Location 1 area)
- The Main Road (Location 2 area) is the transit artery
- Location 1 wins for PEDESTRIAN footfall and walk-in customers
- Location 2 wins for VISIBILITY and vehicular traffic exposure
- For a mobile shop requiring walk-in customers: Location 1 area is better
  (but ground-floor visibility is critical)
- For a sports shop relying on visibility: Location 2 area is better
