1. BUS ROUTES & CONNECTIVITY
================================================================================
  Sankarankovil is a major bus transit hub in Tenkasi district. Key bus routes:

  a) TOWN/VILLAGE BUS ROUTES:
     - Sankarankovil to all surrounding villages (via town bus network)
     - Sankarankovil Bus Depot serves as the main terminus
     - Bus stops on Main Road: Sankarankovil Bus Stop (at Ramalingapuram),
       Main Bus Stand, Bus Depot
     - Nearby village stops: Anaiyoor, Sivalarayanendhal, P.Alangulam,
       Keezha Vayali

  b) INTER-CITY/INTER-STATE ROUTES PASSING THROUGH SANKARANKOVIL:
     - Chennai to Sankarankovil: 48 buses daily, 528 km, ~10.5 hours
       (departures 6:30 PM to 9:30 PM)
     - Tirunelveli to Sankarankovil: Frequent TNSTC buses, first at 6:15 AM
     - Sankarankovil to Bangalore: 4+ daily buses, 421 km, ~10h 40m
     - Tiruppur to Sankarankovil: 17 buses, 216 km, ~5h 40m
     - Madurai to Sankarankovil: Via Dharapuram/Ottanchatiram route
     - Nagercoil to Kumily: Via Tirunelveli, Sankarankovil, Rajapalayam
     - Kumily to Sankarankovil: TNSTC Kumbakonam Route 497 (express)
     - Rajapalayam to Sankarankovil: ~45 minutes, 1 daily bus minimum

  c) PRIVATE BUS OPERATORS:
     - Sivaji, Krishna, Raja (routes to Vellore)
     - Balavinayagar (Vellore to Thanipadi via Tiruvannamalai)
     - Sri Jayavilas Bus Service (Madurai Mattuthavani to Sankarankovil)
     - Jothi Bus Service (local routes, 250km range)
     - Anbukarasi Travels, Midhusha Holidays (from Tirunelveli)

  d) TRANSPORT IMPACT ON FOOTFALL:
     The high volume of inter-city and intra-district buses (48+ to Chennai 
     alone, plus dozens to Tirunelveli, Madurai, Rajapalayam, Tenkasi) creates
     substantial daily commuter and traveler footfall on Main Road.

  a) STATE HIGHWAY 41 (SH-41):
     - Tirunelveli - Rajapalayam - Sankarankovil Road
     - PASSES DIRECTLY THROUGH SANKARANKOVIL TOWN (Main Road is part of SH-41)
     - This is the PRIMARY road the target location sits on
     - Connects to Tirunelveli (major city, ~56 km) and Rajapalayam (~56 km)
     - Part of the broader Tirunelveli-Rajapalayam commerce corridor

  b) STATE HIGHWAY 39 (SH-39):
     - Connects major towns including Srivilliputhur
     - Essential road for local commerce and tourism in Tenkasi district
     - Intersects/feeds into Sankarankovil road network

  c) NATIONAL HIGHWAY 744 (NH-744):
     - Located in Puliyarai Village, Tenkasi district
     - Nearby major national highway for long-distance connectivity

  d) NATIONAL HIGHWAY 44 (NH-44):
     - India's longest highway (Madurai-Tirunelveli stretch nearby)
     - Major north-south corridor of India
     - Accessible via state roads from Sankarankovil

  e) TIRUNELVELI WESTERN BYPASS ROAD:

  f) SANKARANKOVIL BYPASS PROJECT:

  d) BRAHMOTSAVAM & ANNUAL TEMPLE FESTIVALS:
     - Temple celebrates multiple annual festivals
     - "During festivals, footfall significantly increases" - reported sources
     - Pilgrims from both Vaishnavite and Shaivite traditions visit
       (unique temple with half-Shiva, half-Vishnu deity)

  e) FULL MOON / SPECIAL DAYS:

     - Pradosham days: Shiva worship special day
     - Monthly temple events bring regular visitor spikes

  f) WEEKLY PATTERN:

     - Tuesday-Thursday: Steady but lower footfall

10. RELIGIOUS TOURISM: SANKARANARAYANAN TEMPLE VISITOR IMPACT
================================================================================
  a) TEMPLE SIGNIFICANCE:
     - One of the 5 Pancha Bootha Sthalams in Tirunelveli district
     - Built in 10th century AD (historic architecture)
     - Unique deity: Half Shiva and Half Vishnu (only one of its kind)
     - 125-foot tall Rajagopuram with 9 tiers (major landmark)
     - Golden lotus tank is major attraction
     - Dedicated to both Sankara (Shiva) and Narayanan (Vishnu)
     - Both Vaishnavites and Shaivites worship here

  b) VISITOR PATTERN:
     - Devotees from across Tamil Nadu and nearby states visit year-round
     - "Thousands throng every year" during Aadi Thapasu
     - Temple economy "significantly bolsters local trade" (Grokipedia)
     - Temple located in heart of Sankarankovil - adjacent to Main Road
     - Visitors must pass through Main Road commercial corridor

  c) FLOATING POPULATION (OFFICIAL DATA):
     - TNUIFSL Report: "In a day approximately 15,000 floating population 
       is expected in Sankarankovil"
     - This is the DAILY floating population - exceptional for a tier-3 town
     - Temple pilgrims + commercial visitors + transit passengers = 15,000/day
     - Many of these pass through or shop on Main Road

  d) TEMPLE TIMINGS IMPACT:
     - Morning darshan brings early footfall (6 AM onwards)
     - Noon pooja brings mid-day visitors
     - Evening darshan brings peak shopping hours footfall
     - Festival days: Multi-fold increase in all activities

  e) TOWN RANK:
     - Sankarankovil is the 3rd largest town in Tenkasi district
     - Religious tourism is the PRIMARY economic driver
     - Located on eastern slopes of Western Ghats (scenic appeal)
